🔬 Mechanism of Action (LFN verbatim)

"Reactivates cholinesterase (mainly outside CNS) which was inactivated by organophosphate pesticides through the process of phosphorylation. It removes the nerve agent, helping to re-establish normal skeletal muscle contraction and relieve twitching and paralysis of respiratory muscles."

📝 MOA in Clinical Context

Organophosphates inhibit acetylcholinesterase (AChE) via phosphorylation. This prevents ACh breakdown, causing ACh accumulation and cholinergic crisis. Pralidoxime works mainly outside the CNS — it reactivates cholinesterase by removing the phosphate group from the enzyme (dephosphorylation). The LFN MOA specifically mentions two clinical outcomes: (1) re-establishing normal skeletal muscle contraction, and (2) relieving twitching and paralysis of respiratory muscles. These are nicotinic effects — addressed by pralidoxime. Muscarinic effects (secretions, bradycardia, bronchospasm) are addressed by atropine 12.09, not pralidoxime.

Note: The LFN MOA explicitly states "mainly outside CNS" — this is a key qualifier. Pralidoxime does not effectively cross the blood-brain barrier, so central cholinergic effects may require other treatment (e.g., benzodiazepines for seizures).

🤰 Pregnancy Considerations

"In general, medications used as antidotes should take into consideration the health and prognosis of the mother; antidotes should be administered to pregnant women if there is a clear indication for use and should not be withheld because of fears of teratogenicity."

This is the standard LFN antidote pregnancy language — identical to calcium chloride 12.11, calcium gluconate 12.12, and atropine 12.09 OP entries. The framework: maternal indication takes priority; teratogenicity concerns do not justify withholding. No pregnancy-specific dose adjustment is listed.

🩵 Two Indications — Insecticide (Dosed) vs Nerve Agent (PCG Referral)

LFN 12.70 has two distinct indications with completely different formulary handling:

(1) Organophosphate insecticide poisoning: Fully dosed in the formulary. Adult: 1–2 g IV in 100 mL NS over 15–30 min. Peds: 20–50 mg/kg IV in 100 mL NS over 15–30 min (not to exceed adult dosage).

(2) Organophosphate nerve agent poisoning: No standalone dose in the formulary. Both adult and peds entries read: "Dose depends on route and presenting symptoms — refer to Poisoning and Overdose PCG." This PCG referral structure means nerve agent dosing is not self-contained in 12.70 and requires the separate Poisoning and Overdose protocol.

💉 Vehicle: 100 mL NS — Part of the Dose

LFN 12.70 specifies the vehicle: 100 mL NS for both adult and peds. This is part of the dose entry, not a separate mixing instruction. The 100 mL NS vehicle is the same for both adult and peds, even though the drug amount differs. For peds, "dose and volume not to exceed adult dosage" — meaning if the weight-based dose would produce more drug than the adult maximum (1–2 g), cap at the adult dose.

📝 Trade Name: 2-PAM (Not Protopam)

LFN 12.70 lists the trade name as 2-PAM. "Protopam" is a real commercial brand name for pralidoxime chloride found in some pharmacology references, but it is NOT the trade name listed in LFN 12.70. For LFN-specific study, the trade name is 2-PAM. Similarly, the "500 mg/hr continuous infusion" dose seen in some clinical resources is NOT listed in LFN 12.70; the formulary provides only the initial IV dose over 15–30 min for insecticide poisoning.

🚫 CONTRAINDICATION (1)

Hypersensitivity — to pralidoxime or any component. One CI only. No other absolute contraindications listed in LFN 12.70.

⚠️ Warnings / Precautions (3)
Breastfeeding
Use with caution; potential for drug transfer to breast milk; weigh benefit vs risk
Myasthenia Gravis
Pralidoxime may exacerbate myasthenic crisis; cholinesterase reactivation at NMJ in MG may worsen neuromuscular transmission deficits
Renal Impairment
Pralidoxime is renally cleared; impaired elimination → ↑ drug levels; caution and possible dose adjustment in renal dysfunction

These three warnings (breastfeeding, myasthenia gravis, renal impairment) are the complete LFN 12.70 warning list. Rapid-infusion side effects (HTN, tachycardia, muscle rigidity, laryngospasm) appear in the adverse reactions section — not the warnings section — in LFN 12.70's structure.
