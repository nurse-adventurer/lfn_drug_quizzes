🤰 Pregnancy

Recommended for anaphylaxis in pregnancy. Cardiac arrest same as non-pregnant. Should not be withheld due to teratogenicity concerns.

  • IV epi can cause MI in healthy individuals. IM doses are NOT appropriate for IV administration.
  • Push dose epi = temporary/bridge only while preparing an infusion.
  • Doses are concentration dependent. Wrong concentration → medication errors and/or patient harm.
  • Infusion dose range = starting place. Titrate to maintain BP. Consider 2nd agent when approaching max.
  • Concentration ratios retired. 1 mg/mL = 1:1,000; 0.1 mg/mL = 1:10,000; 10 mcg/mL = 1:100,000.

✅ No Absolute Contraindications

Use caution in elderly/debilitated, HTN, DM, hyperthyroidism, Parkinson's, TB, long-standing asthma/emphysema with degenerative heart disease.

🚫 IM ≠ IV — Route Matters

IM doses NOT appropriate for IV. IV epi can cause MI in healthy individuals. 1 mg/mL pushed IV could be lethal.

⚠️ Adverse Reactions

Nervousness, restlessness, tremors, severe headache, CVA, MI, tachydysrhythmias including VF, hypertension, palpitations, precordial pain.

🛑 Traumatic Arrest = Fixed Rate

0.1 mcg/kg/min with NO titrations. Treatment of underlying causes takes priority over epinephrine.
