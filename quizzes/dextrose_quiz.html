🔬 Mechanism of Action

Dextrose is the principal form of glucose (sugar) used by the body to create energy. Since serious brain injury can occur in prolonged hypoglycemia, the rapid administration of glucose is essential. Dextrose 50% IV is the treatment of choice for hypoglycemic patients with an altered mental status or no gag reflex. In hyperkalemia, dextrose paired with insulin drives potassium intracellularly — insulin stimulates the Na+/K+-ATPase pump, moving K+ into cells, and dextrose prevents the iatrogenic hypoglycemia that insulin would otherwise cause.

🤰 Pregnancy

Appropriate use would not be expected to cause adverse developmental outcomes. Maternal hyperglycemia or malnutrition may be associated with adverse pregnancy outcomes. Treat documented hypoglycemia normally in pregnant patients — do not withhold dextrose for hypoglycemic emergencies.

  • For D25, dilute D50 1:1. Draw 25 mL of D50 into a 60 mL syringe, then add 25 mL of normal saline. Result: 50 mL of D25. Used when D10 is unavailable and a middle concentration is needed.
  • For D10, dilute D50 1:4. Waste 50 mL from a 250 mL bag of normal saline, then add 50 mL of D50 to the bag. Result: 250 mL of D10. This is the primary concentration used in LFN dosing.

🧠 THIAMINE + DEXTROSE — Wernicke's Prevention

Per Adverse Reactions: Dextrose can cause severe neurologic symptoms (Wernicke's encephalopathy, Korsakoff's psychosis) if patient is thiamine deficient. Use with 100 mg thiamine in patients suspected of thiamine deficiency. High-risk patients: chronic alcoholism, malnutrition, anorexia, bariatric surgery, prolonged vomiting, dialysis. Give thiamine BEFORE or WITH dextrose — not after.

✅ CONTRAINDICATIONS

None in the emergency setting. This is one of the very few LFN drugs with explicitly NO contraindications. Hypoglycemia is life-threatening — never withhold dextrose for a documented hypoglycemic emergency.

⚠️ Warnings / Precautions (2)

1. May worsen cerebral edema in patients with increasing ICP. Hypertonic dextrose draws water initially, but glucose metabolism and hyperglycemia can worsen cerebral edema. In TBI patients, avoid hyperglycemia — target BGL <180 mg/dL.

2. Extravasation can cause tissue necrosis, especially in high concentrations. D50 is extremely hypertonic (~2,525 mOsm/L). D10 is preferred — less caustic, larger volume allows titration. If using D50, ensure patent large-bore IV.

🚫 Adverse Reactions (Extensive)

Warmth, pain, or burning at injection site. Tissue necrosis, phlebitis, sclerosis, or thrombosis at injection site. Wernicke's encephalopathy and Korsakoff's psychosis if patient is thiamine deficient — use with 100 mg thiamine in suspected deficiency.
