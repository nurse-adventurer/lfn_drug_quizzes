💡 Vecuronium Role in LFN Protocol

Vecuronium is used for paralysis of the already-intubated patient — it is not used for RSI per LFN protocol (succinylcholine and rocuronium are used for RSI per 8.27). Per RSI 8.27, rocuronium 0.5 mg/kg is specified for maintaining paralysis and 1 mg/kg for initiating paralysis. Vecuronium is an alternative non-depolarizing agent at 0.1 mg/kg for ongoing paralysis.

📊 Onset / Peak / Duration

Onset: 2–3 min  |  Peak: Not specified  |  Duration: 30–45 min
Moderate onset (2–3 min) — slower than succinylcholine (30–60 sec) and rocuronium (1 min). Duration of 30–45 min allows repeated dosing PRN to maintain paralysis during transport.

🔬 Mechanism of Action

Blocks acetylcholine from binding to receptors on motor endplate, inhibiting depolarization. This prevents neuromuscular transmission → skeletal muscle paralysis. As a non-depolarizing agent, vecuronium competitively blocks the nicotinic ACh receptor without causing initial depolarization (no fasciculations).

⚠️ Adverse Reactions

Apnea; dysrhythmias; transient hypotension; tachycardia

🤰 Pregnancy Considerations

Data is limited, but its use during pregnancy has been reported.

  • Intubated patients only: Vecuronium is indicated for paralysis of the intubated patient. The patient must have a secured airway before administration — vecuronium causes apnea and the patient will be unable to breathe independently.
  • Sedation and analgesia are mandatory: Per Post-Intubation Sedation 2.08, chemically paralyzed patients must receive sedation AND analgesia. Paralysis without sedation is unacceptable — the patient may be fully aware but unable to move or communicate. Use higher-end dosing ranges for sedation as BP permits.
  • Not used for RSI per LFN: RSI 8.27 specifies succinylcholine (first-line) and rocuronium (alternative) for RSI paralysis. Vecuronium is reserved for maintaining paralysis in the already-intubated patient. Rocuronium 1 mg/kg is used to initiate paralysis in the non-paralyzed intubated patient.
  • May repeat PRN: Unlike succinylcholine (do not repeat dose), vecuronium may be repeated as needed. With a 30–45 min duration, anticipate redosing during extended transports. Monitor for return of muscle tone/movement as cue for redosing.
  • Not necessary on every patient: Per RSI 8.27: "It is not necessary to maintain paralysis on every patient but consider continued paralysis for patients with a history of combativeness or agitation. Consider the constraints of the aircraft/vehicle, patient size, and crew safety."
  • No RASS scores for paralyzed patients: Per Post-Intubation Sedation 2.08 Pearl #5, chemically paralyzed patients should NOT have RASS scores documented. Document "Chemically Paralyzed" under the GCS qualifier. Once no longer paralyzed, resume RASS scoring.

💡 Clinical Context — Paralysis in Transport

Maintaining paralysis during transport prevents patient-ventilator dyssynchrony, unplanned extubation, and dangerous movement in the confined aircraft environment. However, paralysis also eliminates all clinical assessment of consciousness and pain response — making sedation monitoring extremely challenging. BP and HR elevations are indirect measures of distress and must be managed with sedation and analgesia per 2.08.

🚫 CONTRAINDICATIONS

Hypersensitivity to vecuronium or any component of the formulation.

🚫 APNEA — This Drug Causes Respiratory Arrest

Vecuronium produces complete skeletal muscle paralysis including the diaphragm and intercostal muscles. The patient will be unable to breathe. Must have a secured airway and mechanical ventilation before administration. Apnea is listed as the first adverse reaction.

🚫 NEVER Paralyze Without Sedation

Per Post-Intubation Sedation 2.08: Chemically paralyzed patients must receive sedation AND analgesia. Expedite administration of sedatives to avoid paralysis without sedation. Use higher-end dosing ranges as BP permits. BP and HR elevations are indirect measures of distress.

⚠️ No Warnings/Precautions Listed

The LFN formulary does not list specific Warnings/Precautions for vecuronium. However, all neuromuscular blockers require extreme caution: ensure secured airway, continuous monitoring (ECG, SpO2, ETCO2), and adequate sedation/analgesia.

⚠️ Adverse Reactions

Apnea; dysrhythmias; transient hypotension; tachycardia. Monitor cardiac rhythm continuously. Transient hypotension may occur — be prepared to treat hemodynamic changes. Tachycardia may be a sign of inadequate sedation/analgesia in the paralyzed patient.

🤰 Pregnancy Considerations

Data is limited, but its use during pregnancy has been reported. Not contraindicated in pregnancy per LFN formulary.

📋 Documentation — Paralyzed Patients

Per 2.08 Pearl #5: Chemically paralyzed patients should NOT have RASS scores documented. Document "Chemically Paralyzed" under the GCS qualifier. Resume RASS scoring once no longer paralyzed.
