STEMI Weight Tiers vs Stroke Weight-Based

STEMI uses tiered flat doses by weight band. Stroke uses a continuous weight-based calculation (0.25 mg/kg, capped at 25 mg). These are different dosing systems — do not apply stroke math to STEMI or vice versa.

Pregnancy

Bleeding may occur with tenecteplase therapy and the risk of bleeding complications may be increased in pregnant patients; however, use may be considered if other treatment options are not feasible or appropriate.

Special Considerations — Reconstitution & Administration

SC 1 — Reconstituting and Administering Tenecteplase (TNKase)

  • Using the syringe, needle, and Sterile Water supplied, draw the full 10 mL sterile water into the syringe
  • DO NOT use bacteriostatic water
  • Inject the 10 mL sterile water into the TNKase, directing the stream into the powder. There may be some foaming. Allow the vial to settle until bubbles dissipate
  • Swirl the vial gently until contents are completely dissolved. DO NOT shake vial
  • Final concentration is 50 mg/10 mL. Liquid should be clear and colorless/pale yellow
  • Draw the appropriate volume into the syringe. Leave extra solution in the vial to avoid inadvertent overdosing
  • Administer TNKase as a bolus over 5 seconds via a dedicated line
  • Tenecteplase is incompatible with dextrose — flush dextrose-containing lines with NS before and after administration

Warnings, Contraindications & Adverse Reactions

Contraindications (per LFN 12.79)

  • Active internal bleeding
  • Intracranial surgery or trauma within last 2 months
  • Intracranial aneurysm
  • Coagulation disorder
  • Severe uncontrolled HTN
  • Recent GI/GU bleeding

Warnings / Precautions

  • Coronary thrombolysis may result in reperfusion arrhythmias
  • Internal/external bleeding may occur
  • Hypersensitivity reactions have been reported — monitor closely during infusion and for several hours after

Adverse Reactions

Intracranial hemorrhage, stroke, bleeding, arrhythmia, reperfusion, anaphylaxis, angioedema, cholesterol embolism, hypersensitivity reaction

Key Points — LFN 12.79

  • Two indications: STEMI and Ischemic Stroke
  • STEMI: tiered weight-band dosing (30/35/40/45/50 mg by 10 kg bands) — single bolus over 5 seconds
  • Stroke: 0.25 mg/kg IV, max 25 mg — single bolus over 5 seconds
  • MOA: binds to fibrin, converts tissue plasminogen to plasmin, promoting fibrinolysis
  • Trade name: TNKase — final concentration 50 mg/10 mL
  • Reconstitution: 10 mL sterile water only (not bacteriostatic); swirl, do not shake
  • Administration: dedicated line, bolus over 5 sec; incompatible with dextrose — flush with NS
  • 6 contraindications including active internal bleeding, intracranial aneurysm, coagulation disorder
  • Most significant adverse reaction: intracranial hemorrhage (listed first)
  • Warning: reperfusion arrhythmias and hypersensitivity reactions — monitor during infusion and several hours after
  • Pregnancy: bleeding risk increased; use may be considered if no other options
