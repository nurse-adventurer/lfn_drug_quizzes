🔬 Mechanism of Action

Selectively inhibits cyclic adenosine monophosphate phosphodiesterase (cAMP) isoenzyme found in cardiac and vascular muscle. This activity leads to increase of intracellular ionized calcium and contractile force in heart muscle.

🤰 Pregnancy

Adverse events have not been observed in animal reproduction studies; however, increased resorption was reported in some studies.

💉 Inodilator — Dual Mechanism

Milrinone is called an "inodilator" because it provides both inotropy (↑ contractility) and vasodilation (↓ SVR, ↓ PVR). Unlike dobutamine, it works independent of beta-adrenergic receptors — meaning it remains effective in patients on chronic beta-blockers.

⏱️ Long Duration — Plan Ahead

Duration is 4 hours after stopping. Effects persist long after the infusion ends. This is not like dobutamine (2-min half-life). If hypotension develops from milrinone, stopping the drip will not produce an immediate reversal — vasopressor support must be available and started promptly.

🩺 CLINICAL CONTEXT — CHF with High Afterload

The failing heart is often stuck pumping against elevated SVR. Milrinone's dual action directly addresses this: it makes the heart squeeze harder (↑ Ca²⁺) while simultaneously reducing the resistance it pumps against (↓ SVR, ↓ PVR). This is particularly useful in RV failure from pulmonary hypertension, where reducing PVR is just as important as improving RV contractility.

🔄 RENAL IMPAIRMENT — Dose Consideration

Milrinone is renally excreted. Per LFN 12.55: use caution with renal impairment. Impaired clearance extends the already-long 4-hour duration, increasing risk of cumulative hypotension and arrhythmias. Consider dose reduction or alternative agents (e.g., dobutamine) in significant AKI.

🚫 CONTRAINDICATION

Hypersensitivity to milrinone or any component of the formulation.

⚠️ Use with Caution — Structural Cardiac Disease

Severe aortic stenosis; pulmonic valve disease; hypertrophic cardiomyopathy. In fixed outflow obstructions (AS, HOCM), milrinone's vasodilation can drop preload and afterload simultaneously without a corresponding increase in flow through the fixed obstruction — worsening the hemodynamic picture.

⚠️ Use with Caution — Renal Impairment and Atrial Fib/Flutter

Renal impairment: Milrinone is renally excreted — reduced clearance extends duration and increases toxicity risk. Atrial fib/flutter: Milrinone may enhance AV conduction, potentially increasing ventricular rate in patients with AF or flutter without adequate rate control.

🚫 Adverse Reactions (3)

Hypotension; ventricular arrhythmias; headache.

• Hypotension — from vasodilation (↓ SVR); loading dose is higher risk; have vasopressor (norepinephrine) available
• Ventricular arrhythmias — ↑ cAMP → ↑ intracellular Ca²⁺ → increased automaticity, triggered activity; risk higher in ischemic myocardium
• Headache — vasodilatory side effect
