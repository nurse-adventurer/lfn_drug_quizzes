🦗 Terbutaline

LFN Drug Formulary Section 12.80 · Clinical Application Quiz

Beta-2 Agonist / Bronchodilator / Sympathomimetic
Preterm Labor (Adult) Asthma in Extremis (Peds) Peds: SQ/IM/IV Tocolytic Mixture: 1 mg/100 mL

Dosing & Indications

IndicationAdult DosePediatric Dose
Preterm Labor 0.25 mg SQ
• Repeat q 30 min until tocolysis achieved or max of 3 doses
• Hold if HR >120 		No LFN Standing Order
Asthma in Extremis No LFN Standing Order 10 mcg/kg SQ/IM OR 10 mcg/kg IV over 10 min
• Maximum 400 mcg/dose SC/IM/IV
• May repeat q 20 min PRN, max 3 total doses

Infusion (after initial bolus):
• Start at 0.4 mcg/kg/min
• Titrate by 0.1–0.5 mcg/kg/min q 30 min
• Max 5 mcg/kg/min
• Requires consult with sending/receiving provider

LFN Standing Order Structure — Critical Distinction

  • Adult: Preterm labor only — NO adult standing order for asthma
  • Pediatric: Asthma in extremis only — NO pedi standing order for preterm labor
  • Preterm labor repeat interval is q30 min (not q20 min)
  • Recommended mixture: 1 mg / 100 mL

Pharmacology

Mechanism of Action
Stimulates adenyl cyclase resulting in increased levels of intracellular AMP. Elevated cAMP triggers relaxation of bronchial smooth muscle and inhibition of release of inflammatory mediators
Pharmacologic Category
Beta-2 agonist, bronchodilator, sympathomimetic
Onset / Peak / Duration
15–30 min / variable / 2–5 hours
Trade Name
Brethine
Recommended Mixture
1 mg / 100 mL
Adverse Reactions
Palpitations, tachyarrhythmias, hyperglycemia, hypokalemia, headache, seizure, tremor, nervous-feeling, muscle cramps, weakness, paradoxical bronchospasm, pulmonary edema

Pregnancy

Terbutaline crosses the placenta. Terbutaline may affect uterine contractility; use caution if needed to control bronchospasm in pregnant patients. Terbutaline has been used in the management of preterm labor.

Special Considerations

Preterm Labor — Hold Parameter

LFN 12.80 lists "Hold if HR >120" directly in the dosing table for preterm labor. This is a built-in protocol instruction, not a separate special consideration section.

Pediatric Infusion (Asthma in Extremis)

After initial bolus dose, a continuous infusion may be initiated:

  • Start at 0.4 mcg/kg/min
  • Titrate by 0.1–0.5 mcg/kg/min q 30 min PRN
  • Max dose: 5 mcg/kg/min
  • Requires consult with sending/receiving provider
  • Mixture: 1 mg / 100 mL NS (10 mcg/mL)

Warnings, Contraindications & Adverse Reactions

Contraindications (per LFN 12.80)

Hypersensitivity

Warnings / Precautions

Caution in cardiovascular disease; uncontrolled HTN; hypokalemia; diabetes.

Note: Cardiovascular disease is a caution in LFN 12.80, not a contraindication. The only listed contraindication is hypersensitivity.

Adverse Reactions

Palpitations, tachyarrhythmias, hyperglycemia, hypokalemia, headache, seizure, tremor, nervous-feeling, muscle cramps, weakness, paradoxical bronchospasm, pulmonary edema

Key Points — LFN 12.80

  • Adult: Preterm labor only — NO adult standing order for asthma
  • Pediatric: Asthma in extremis only — no pedi preterm labor order
  • Preterm labor: 0.25 mg SQ · repeat q30 min · max 3 doses · Hold if HR >120
  • Pedi asthma: 10 mcg/kg SQ/IM or IV over 10 min · max 400 mcg · q20 min · max 3 doses
  • Pedi infusion: 0.4 mcg/kg/min start, max 5 mcg/kg/min — requires provider consult
  • MOA: stimulates adenyl cyclase → increased intracellular AMP → bronchial smooth muscle relaxation + inhibits inflammatory mediators
  • Category: Beta-2 agonist, bronchodilator, sympathomimetic
  • Trade: Brethine · Mixture: 1 mg/100 mL
  • Onset: 15–30 min / Peak: variable / Duration: 2–5 hours
  • Contraindication: hypersensitivity only — cardiovascular disease/HTN are warnings, not CI
  • Key adverse reactions: tachyarrhythmias, tremor, hypokalemia, hyperglycemia, paradoxical bronchospasm, pulmonary edema, seizure
