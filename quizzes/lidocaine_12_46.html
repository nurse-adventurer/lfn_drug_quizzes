Mechanism of Action

Suppresses automaticity in the His-Purkinje system and by elevating electrical stimulation threshold of ventricle during diastole. As a Class Ib antiarrhythmic, it stabilizes ventricular myocardium by blocking sodium channels in their inactivated state.

Adverse Reactions

Drowsiness, dizziness, light-headedness. At toxic levels: seizures, respiratory depression, cardiovascular collapse.

  1. Doses and indications should follow current ACLS guidelines. Lidocaine is an alternative to amiodarone for VF/pulseless VT.
  2. Post-ROSC infusion (1–4 mg/min) should only be initiated if ROSC was obtained after a lidocaine bolus during resuscitation.
  3. For tachycardias, may repeat 0.5–0.75 mg/kg every 3–5 minutes to a maximum of 3 doses or 3 mg/kg total.
  4. IO Pain: Administer slowly (120 seconds) and allow 60-second dwell time before flushing for local anesthetic effect.
  5. For chest tube local anesthesia, use fanning injection technique to cover a diffuse area. Lidocaine vials are not sterile — do not contact sterile field or gloves.
💡 Clinical Context — Cardiac Arrest

Lidocaine vs Amiodarone: Both are used for refractory VF/pulseless VT. Lidocaine is an alternative antiarrhythmic per current ACLS guidelines. The initial dose is 1–1.5 mg/kg with repeat doses of 0.5–0.75 mg/kg.

Post-ROSC strategy: If ROSC was achieved after a lidocaine bolus, initiate maintenance infusion at 1–4 mg/min to prevent recurrent arrhythmia. Use recommended mixture of 1 gram / 250 mL (4 mg/mL).

🔑 IO Pain Management Key Points

Adults and peds >20 kg: 40 mg IO over 120 seconds. Allow to dwell for 60 seconds before flushing.

Peds <20 kg: 0.5 mg/kg IO over 120 seconds. Allow to dwell 60 seconds. Maximum dose 40 mg.

Mechanism: Slow administration and dwell time allow lidocaine to provide local anesthetic effect at the IO site before flushing with fluids.

🩺 Chest Tube Local Anesthesia

Inject up to 5 mL of 2% solution, fanning out with injection to cover diffuse area. Max dose 4 mg/kg.

Pediatric (<25 kg): Max dose of 0.2 mL/kg.

Technique: Inject subcutaneously under the skin, advance to bone and inject periosteum of the rib above, then move superior and inject the intercostal muscle. Lidocaine vials are not sterile — should not contact sterile field or gloves.

Pregnancy Considerations

Doses and indications should follow current ACLS guidelines. Appropriate medications should not be withheld due to concerns of fetal teratogenicity.

🚨 CONTRAINDICATION — Amide-Type Hypersensitivity

Contraindicated in patients with a history of hypersensitivity to amide-type local anesthetics. Cross-reactivity may occur with other amide agents (bupivacaine, mepivacaine, prilocaine, ropivacaine). Obtain allergy history before administration.

🚨 CONTRAINDICATION — Supraventricular Dysrhythmias

Contraindicated in patients with supraventricular dysrhythmias (SVT). Lidocaine is selective for ventricular tissue and is ineffective — and potentially harmful — in supraventricular arrhythmias. Always confirm rhythm is ventricular in origin before administering.

🚨 CONTRAINDICATION — Heart Block

Contraindicated in patients with heart block. Lidocaine suppresses automaticity and can worsen conduction delays, potentially causing complete heart block or asystole in patients with existing conduction abnormalities.

⚠️ Liver or Renal Disease

Use with caution in patients with liver or renal disease. Lidocaine is hepatically metabolized. Impaired liver function reduces clearance, increasing risk of toxicity. Reduce dose or extend intervals in patients with significant hepatic impairment.

⚠️ CHF, Hypoxia & Respiratory Depression

Use with caution in patients with CHF, marked hypoxia, respiratory depression, hypovolemia, or shock. These conditions reduce hepatic blood flow, impairing lidocaine metabolism and increasing toxicity risk. Hypoxia also lowers the seizure threshold for lidocaine toxicity.

⚠️ Myasthenia Gravis & Debilitated Patients

Use with caution in patients with myasthenia gravis, debilitated patients, and the elderly. Lidocaine may potentiate neuromuscular weakness. Elderly patients have reduced hepatic clearance and may require dose adjustments.

⚠️ Malignant Hyperthermia Risk

Use with caution in patients with a family history of malignant hyperthermia (fulminant hypermetabolism). While amide local anesthetics have not been definitively linked to triggering MH, caution is warranted in susceptible individuals.

⚠️ Drug Interaction — Metoprolol

Metoprolol may increase serum concentration of lidocaine. Beta-blockers reduce hepatic blood flow, impairing lidocaine clearance. Monitor for signs of toxicity (drowsiness, dizziness, seizures) when co-administering.
