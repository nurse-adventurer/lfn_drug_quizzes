🔬 Mechanism of Action

Selectively prevents hypersynchronization of epileptiform burst firing. Exact action unknown. Unlike benzos (GABA enhancement) or fosphenytoin (sodium channel blockade), levetiracetam works through a unique and incompletely understood mechanism — it binds to SV2A (synaptic vesicle protein 2A), modulating neurotransmitter release. This different target explains why it can work when benzos and fosphenytoin fail.

⚡ SEIZURE TREATMENT LADDER — Where Levetiracetam Fits

Step 1 (First-line): Benzodiazepines — midazolam 0.1 mg/kg IV/IM/IN, diazepam 5-10 mg IV, or lorazepam 2-4 mg IV. GABA enhancement.

Step 2: Fosphenytoin 15-20 mg PE/kg at 150 mg PE/min (12.32). Na+ channel blockade.

Step 3: Levetiracetam 50 mg/kg over 15 min (12.45). SV2A modulation. Per SC #1: "persistent seizures with consultation of sending/receiving physician."

Step 4 (Refractory): Ketamine 1 mg/kg q 5 min, max 5 mg/kg (12.42). NMDA antagonism.

Step 5: Phenobarbital 15-20 mg/kg at 60 mg/min (12.67). Barbiturate.

🤰 Pregnancy

Levetiracetam crosses the placenta and can be detected in the newborn. Maternal polytherapy with antiseizure drugs may increase congenital malformation risk; monotherapy with lowest effective dose is recommended. However, untreated status epilepticus is more dangerous than the drug — treat SE aggressively regardless of pregnancy status.

🏥 SC #1 — PERSISTENT SEIZURES WITH PHYSICIAN CONSULT

Should be considered for persistent seizures with consultation of sending/receiving physician. This means levetiracetam is NOT a standing-order first-line anticonvulsant — it requires physician consultation before administration. It's used when first-line agents (benzos) have failed and the patient needs escalation. The physician consult requirement reflects that this is a step-up in the treatment ladder.

🏥 SC #2 — MUST BE OBTAINED FROM SENDING FACILITY

Must be obtained from the sending facility. Levetiracetam is NOT stocked on LFN aircraft. You must request it from the hospital pharmacy before transport. Plan ahead: if you anticipate needing levetiracetam during transport, obtain it during the facility assessment. Ensure the dose is calculated and mixed before departure.

💊 ADMINISTRATION — Mix in 100 mL NS

Mix in 100 mL NS bag, infuse over 15 minutes. This is a simple preparation — add the calculated dose to a 100 mL NS bag and run it over 15 min. For a 3000 mg dose, that's adding 3 vials (1000 mg each) or equivalent to the bag. The dilution reduces infusion-related reactions.

🚫 CONTRAINDICATIONS (1)

Known hypersensitivity. Simple CI — only hypersensitivity. No hemodynamic restrictions, no age restrictions, no disease-state CIs (unlike fosphenytoin which is CI in AV block and bradycardia). This favorable safety profile is one reason it's increasingly used in seizure management.

⚠️ Warnings / Precautions (1)

Renal failure and creatinine clearance. Decrease dose by 50%. Levetiracetam is primarily renally excreted (~66% unchanged in urine). In renal failure, clearance drops → drug accumulates → excessive CNS depression. The 50% dose reduction prevents accumulation-related toxicity.

🚫 Adverse Reactions (3)

Drowsiness; weakness; headache. All are CNS effects consistent with an anticonvulsant. Notably ABSENT: no significant hemodynamic effects (no hypotension like fosphenytoin), no respiratory depression (unlike phenobarbital), no cardiac conduction issues. This clean adverse profile makes levetiracetam relatively safe compared to other anticonvulsants.
