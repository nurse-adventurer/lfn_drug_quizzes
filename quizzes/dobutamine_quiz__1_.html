💡 Titration Pattern — Gentle Increases

Unlike most LFN vasopressors that just say "titrate q 3–5 min," dobutamine specifies a titration increment: 0.5–1 mcg/kg/min per step. This reflects the drug's potent inotropic effects and narrow therapeutic window in a compromised heart.

🔬 Mechanism of Action

Increases cardiac output (positive inotropic effect) and decreases pulmonary wedge pressure and total systemic vascular resistance. Primary use: inotropic support for depressed myocardial contractility (cardiogenic shock) from organic heart disease or cardiac surgery. Increases conduction through the AV node.

🤰 Pregnancy

May be considered post-resuscitation. Effects of inotropic support on the fetus should be considered. Should not be withheld due to teratogenicity concerns.

  • Should NOT be used as sole agent in hypovolemic shock unless fluid resuscitation is well underway. Dobutamine's vasodilatory effect (↓ SVR) can worsen hypotension in volume-depleted patients.

🚫 HYPOVOLEMIC SHOCK — When NOT to Use Dobutamine Alone

SC #1 in action: A hypotensive trauma patient with ongoing hemorrhage is NOT a dobutamine candidate. Their problem is empty tank (hypovolemia), not a broken pump (cardiogenic shock). Dobutamine's ↓ SVR would further drop BP in an already volume-depleted patient. Give fluids/blood first, then vasopressors. Only consider dobutamine if pump failure is the primary problem AND the patient is refractory to other interventions.

🚫 CONTRAINDICATIONS

Hypersensitivity; ventricular tachycardia. The VT contraindication reflects dobutamine's ability to increase AV conduction and potentially worsen arrhythmias.

⚠️ Use With Caution

Chronic hypertension or atrial fibrillation. Dobutamine increases AV conduction — in afib, this can accelerate the ventricular response rate.

🚫 May Increase Severity/Size of Infarctions

Dobutamine increases myocardial oxygen demand by increasing contractility and heart rate. In a patient with active MI, this can extend infarct size. Weigh the risk of worsening infarction against the benefit of improving pump function.

⚠️ Adverse Reactions

May increase severity/size of infarctions; dysrhythmias; hypotension; tissue necrosis with extravasation. The hypotension paradox: an inotrope that can cause hypotension — because ↓ SVR may outpace ↑ CO.

📋 Tissue Necrosis Risk

Like norepinephrine, dobutamine can cause tissue necrosis with extravasation. Ensure large, patent IV access. Monitor the IV site closely during infusion.
