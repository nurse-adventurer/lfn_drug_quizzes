👶 PEDIATRIC DOSING

0.5–1 g/kg IV (weight-based, not flat dose). Example: 28 kg child = 14–28 g. Adults get flat 25 g doses; pediatrics are weight-based. No maximum specified in protocol, but clinical judgment guides repeat dosing.

🔬 Mechanism of Action

Increases intravascular oncotic (colloid osmotic) pressure. Albumin molecules are too large to cross capillary membranes easily — they create an osmotic gradient that pulls water FROM the interstitial space INTO blood vessels, expanding plasma volume. Albumin accounts for ~80% of normal plasma oncotic pressure. Each gram holds approximately 18 mL of fluid intravascularly.

🤰 Pregnancy

Use during pregnancy is not expected to result in significant exposure to the fetus. Albumin is a large protein molecule that does not readily cross the placenta. Safe to use when indicated for maternal hypovolemia or hypoalbuminemia.

  • 25% concentration should be considered in patients where sodium or fluid restriction is needed. CHF, renal failure, and volume-overloaded patients benefit from the hyperoncotic "pull" effect with minimal administered volume.
  • Albumin can be used as an adjunct to exchange transfusion in severe neonatal hyperbilirubinemia — albumin binds bilirubin, pulling it into the intravascular space where it can be removed during exchange. Each albumin molecule binds ~2 bilirubin molecules.
  • Color may range from nearly colorless to brownish — this is normal variation based on protein concentration and manufacturing. NOT an indication of contamination. Turbidity, particles, or leaking containers ARE reasons to discard.

🚫 CRITICAL — TBI (Traumatic Brain Injury)

The use of albumin in traumatic brain injury is associated with higher mortality. SAFE-TBI substudy: 24.5% mortality (albumin) vs 15.1% (saline) = 63% higher death rate. Albumin crosses the disrupted blood-brain barrier → increases local oncotic pressure → worsens cerebral edema → higher ICP. Use crystalloids or blood products for TBI resuscitation.

🚫 Contraindications

Hypersensitivity to albumin or any component. Albumin is derived from pooled human plasma — trace contaminants and stabilizers (sodium caprylate, acetyltryptophan) can trigger reactions.

⚠️ Adverse Reactions

Anaphylaxis; chills; fever; flushing; urticaria; nausea/vomiting; tachycardia. Anaphylaxis is the most serious — stop infusion immediately, epinephrine IM, supportive care. IgA-deficient patients may be at higher risk.

📋 Storage & Appearance

Store at room temperature (20–25°C). Do NOT freeze. Protect from light. Once opened, use within 4 hours. Color ranges from nearly colorless to brownish — this is normal. Discard if turbid, contains particles, or container is damaged.
