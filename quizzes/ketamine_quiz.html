🔬 Mechanism of Action — Dual Nature

Releases catecholamines and stimulates the sympathetic nervous system. Has powerful analgesic and sedative properties and directly relaxes bronchial smooth muscle producing bronchodilation. Minimal effects on respiratory drive.

⚠️ Direct myocardial depressant effect normally masked by sympathomimetic effects — UNMASKED when catecholamines depleted (sepsis, acidosis, CHF, prolonged shock, stimulant intoxication)

🤰 Pregnancy

Ketamine crosses the placenta. Low doses may be used when sedation/analgesia needed for procedures during pregnancy, but other agents are preferred.

  1. Continuous monitoring of ECG, SpO₂, and ETCO₂ required for ALL patients receiving ketamine.
  2. Infusions should only be used on intubated patients.
  3. Larson's maneuver for laryngospasm: Press middle finger behind earlobe firmly inward/upward toward base of skull + jaw thrust. Laryngospasm is transient — continue to oxygenate/ventilate.
  4. Increased muscle tone with tonic/clonic movements resembling seizures: Occurs with initial dose, gradually decreases. Typically does NOT affect chest wall or respiration. NOT an allergic reaction, NOT a true seizure. Treatment rarely needed.
  5. Refractory seizures: Reserved for patients who fail to respond to multiple doses of benzodiazepines.

💡 Post-Intub Context (PCG 2.08)

Ketamine used primarily for hypotensive patients or agitated patients where benzos are CI. Consider reducing dose in catecholamine-depleted states. Emergence phenomenon: give benzo to blunt; more common in adults than peds.

🚨 Hypotensive RSI (PCG 8.27)

If SBP <110 (head injuries) or <90 (all others): MUST hemodynamically resuscitate PRIOR to RSI meds. Use half-dose ketamine (0.5-1 mg/kg) or half-dose midazolam for induction.

🚫 CONTRAINDICATIONS (3)

1. Age <3 months (unless intubated) — immature airway reflexes + high laryngospasm risk.
2. Schizophrenia — dissociative/psychotomimetic effects exacerbate psychosis.
3. Conditions where increased BP would be harmful — sympathomimetic effects cause HTN/tachycardia. Use caution if SBP >180.

🚨 HIGH-RISK STATES — Catecholamine Depletion (The "Ketamine Crash")

Ketamine's hemodynamic "stability" depends on intact catecholamine reserves. When depleted, direct myocardial depression is unmasked → cardiovascular collapse.

⚠️ Adverse Reactions (7)

Laryngospasm; increased muscle tone; nausea/vomiting; enhanced protective airway reflexes; excessive salivation; hypertension; tachycardia. Laryngospasm: Larson's maneuver (SC #3). Muscle tone/tonic-clonic: NOT seizures, NOT allergy (SC #4). Salivation: atropine 0.25 mg IV PRN (12.09).
