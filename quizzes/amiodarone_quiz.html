🔬 Mechanism of Action

Prolongs duration of the action potential and refractory period without significantly affecting the resting membrane potential. Relaxes vascular smooth muscle, decreases peripheral vascular resistance, and increases coronary blood flow. This is a multi-channel blocker: blocks sodium (Class I), potassium (Class III), calcium (Class IV), and has anti-adrenergic properties (Class II). The most broad-spectrum antiarrhythmic in the formulary.

🤰 Pregnancy

Oral or IV amiodarone should be used in pregnancy only to treat arrhythmias refractory to other treatments or when other treatments are contraindicated. Not first-line in pregnancy — reserve for life-threatening situations where alternatives have failed.

  • Give UNDILUTED in cardiac arrest. In pulseless VT/VF, push the 300 mg rapidly — no time for dilution, no BP to protect. Speed matters.
  • Dilute with D5W to 1.8 mg/mL (450 mg/250 mL or 900 mg/500 mL) for stable patients. The slow infusion rate prevents hypotension.
  • 0.9% saline may be used if D5W not available. D5W is preferred diluent, but NS works in a pinch.
📊 24-HOUR DOSE TRACKING — Know Your Running Total

Typical 24-hr total with full arrest dosing + maintenance:
Arrest boluses: 300 + 150 = 450 mg
Phase 1 infusion: 1 mg/min × 6 hr = 360 mg
Phase 2 infusion: 0.5 mg/min × 18 hr = 540 mg
Total: ~1350 mg (well under 2.2 g max)

If only stable tachycardia boluses (150 mg each) + infusion, total is even lower. Track ALL doses given PTA + during transport + communicated at handoff.

💊 BETA-BLOCKER INTERACTION — From Metoprolol SC #1

Amiodarone may enhance the bradycardic effect of beta-blockers, possibly to the point of cardiac arrest. Amiodarone may also increase beta-blocker serum concentrations. If the patient is already on a beta-blocker (metoprolol, esmolol), amiodarone adds to AV node suppression and negative chronotropy. Monitor closely for profound bradycardia.

🚫 CONTRAINDICATIONS (3)

Hypersensitivity; bradycardia; cardiogenic shock. Do not give amiodarone to a patient who is already bradycardic (it slows conduction further) or in cardiogenic shock (it decreases contractility and causes vasodilation).

⚠️ Warnings / Precautions

Avoid in QT prolongation. Amiodarone blocks K⁺ channels → ↑ QT interval → risk of torsades de pointes. If QTc is already prolonged (>500 ms), amiodarone can worsen it. May decrease automaticity, conductivity, and contractility — triple negative cardiac effects.

🚫 Adverse Reactions (4)

Bradycardia; hypotension; asystole; nausea/vomiting. Hypotension is most common with rapid IV administration (especially in patients with a pulse). The solvent system (polysorbate 80) contributes to vasodilation. Asystole = the most feared adverse reaction — complete cessation of cardiac activity.

⚠️ Torsades de Pointes — Use with Extreme Caution

Amiodarone's Class III (K⁺ channel) effect prolongs the QT interval. In patients with existing QT prolongation or torsades de pointes, amiodarone can worsen the arrhythmia. Correct treatment for torsades is MgSO₄ + overdrive pacing, NOT more QT-prolonging drugs. Check K⁺ and Mg²⁺ before amiodarone when possible.

📋 Iodine Content — Thyroid Effects

Amiodarone is 37% iodine by weight. Can cause both hyper- and hypothyroidism. A 200 mg dose delivers ~75 mg of organic iodine (daily requirement is only 150–200 μg). Use caution in patients with known thyroid disease. Long-term effects, but relevant for patient handoff communication.
