📊 Onset / Peak / Duration

Onset: 30 min  |  Peak: 2 hours  |  Duration: ~110 hours (90% inhibition of platelet aggregation)
Rapid oral onset supports early STEMI intervention. Long duration (~4.6 days) from reversible but sustained receptor binding.

🔬 Mechanism of Action

Reversibly and noncompetitively binds the adenosine diphosphate (ADP) P2Y12 receptor on the platelet surface, which prevents ADP-mediated activation of the GPIIb/IIIa receptor complex, thereby reducing platelet aggregation.

🤰 Pregnancy Considerations

Due to lack of data, use in pregnancy is not recommended.

  • STEMI-specific indication: Per LFN protocol 12.81, ticagrelor is indicated solely for STEMI. It is given as a 180 mg PO loading dose as part of dual antiplatelet therapy (DAPT) alongside aspirin 324 mg PO (per 12.08).
  • Oral administration: PO route only. No IV formulation. No recommended mixture — this is a tablet, not an infusion. If patient cannot swallow, tablets can be crushed per standard antiplatelet protocols.
  • Reversible P2Y12 inhibitor: Unlike clopidogrel (irreversible), ticagrelor binds reversibly. This means platelet function recovers more predictably when the drug is cleared (~110 hours for 90% inhibition). No prodrug activation required — no genetic variability concerns (unlike CYP2C19-dependent clopidogrel).
  • Rapid onset for STEMI: Onset of 30 minutes (oral) is relatively fast for antiplatelet therapy. Peak effect at 2 hours. Supports early PCI intervention by providing rapid platelet inhibition.
  • Cardiac conduction cautions: Per Warnings/Precautions, use caution in sick sinus syndrome and 2nd or 3rd degree AV block without pacemaker. Ticagrelor may cause bradycardia via adenosine-related mechanisms. ECG monitoring is prudent.
  • No LFN pediatric standing order: No pediatric dosing established per LFN protocol.

💡 Clinical Context — Why Dual Antiplatelet?

STEMI results from acute coronary thrombosis. Aspirin and ticagrelor target two different platelet activation pathways — COX/thromboxane A2 and ADP/P2Y12 respectively. Dual antiplatelet therapy (DAPT) provides synergistic inhibition of platelet aggregation, reducing risk of re-occlusion and stent thrombosis after PCI.

🚫 CONTRAINDICATIONS

Hypersensitivity to ticagrelor or any component; active bleeding; history of intracranial hemorrhage (ICH); CABG surgery; severe hepatic impairment.

🚫 HEMORRHAGE — Most Serious Adverse Reaction

Hemorrhage is listed as an adverse reaction. As an antiplatelet agent, ticagrelor increases bleeding risk. Active bleeding of any type is an absolute contraindication. History of ICH — even remote — is also an absolute contraindication due to the devastating consequences of re-bleeding.

⚠️ Caution — High-Risk Populations

Per LFN formulary Warnings/Precautions: Caution in elderly patients; recent elective surgery; sick sinus syndrome; 2nd or 3rd degree AV block without pacemaker.

⚠️ Cardiac Conduction — Sick Sinus & AV Block

Ticagrelor may affect cardiac conduction via adenosine-related mechanisms. Use caution in patients with sick sinus syndrome or 2nd/3rd degree AV block without pacemaker. Monitor ECG and heart rate. Bradycardia has been reported.

⚠️ Adverse Reactions

Dyspnea — commonly reported with ticagrelor, potentially related to adenosine reuptake inhibition. Also: nausea, hemorrhage, dizziness.

🚫 CABG Surgery — Contraindicated

CABG surgery is listed as a contraindication. Perioperative antiplatelet therapy in CABG significantly increases surgical bleeding risk. Ticagrelor should be held before planned CABG.

🤰 Pregnancy Considerations

Due to lack of data, use in pregnancy is not recommended.

💊 Administration

180 mg PO (oral loading dose). No IV formulation available. No recommended mixture — this is a tablet medication.
