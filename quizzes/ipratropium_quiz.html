🔬 Mechanism of Action

Anticholinergic agent, chemically related to atropine. Given in a nebulized form, it acts directly on the smooth muscle of the bronchial tree by inhibiting acetylcholine at receptor sites. By blocking parasympathetic action, it dilates the bronchial smooth muscle and decreases secretions. Two effects: (1) bronchodilation by blocking vagally-mediated bronchoconstriction, and (2) decreased secretions by blocking mucous gland stimulation. Complementary to albuterol which works through beta-2 stimulation.

🤰 Pregnancy

Systemic exposure following inhalation is negligible; maternal use is not expected to result in fetal exposure. This is one of the safest pregnancy statements in the formulary — the inhaled route means almost no systemic absorption, so fetal exposure is essentially zero.

🚫 SC #1 — NOT PRIMARY TREATMENT FOR ACUTE BRONCHOSPASM

It should not be used as the primary treatment for acute episodes of bronchospasm, for which a rapid response is required. This is because ipratropium's onset (5-15 min) is slower than albuterol (<5 min). In acute severe bronchospasm, the patient needs the FASTEST bronchodilation possible = albuterol first. Ipratropium is an ADJUNCT that provides additional bronchodilation through a different pathway, not the primary agent.

🫁 PCG 5.04 ASTHMA/COPD TREATMENT CONTEXT

Asthma ALS Adult:
1. Albuterol 2.5 mg + ipratropium 0.5 mg via neb (DuoNeb when available)
2. May repeat q 10 min PRN
3. After 2nd dose of ipratropium → continuous albuterol treatments
4. Methylprednisolone 125 mg IV/IM
5. Epinephrine 0.3 mg IM (severe symptoms)
6. Magnesium sulfate 2 g over 20 min (persistent severe/prolonged transport)

COPD ALS Adult: Same first 3 steps + methylprednisolone. Consider NIV (CPAP/BiPAP). Target SpO₂ 90-94% for COPD.

💊 DUONEB — The Combination Product

Per albuterol 12.05 SC #7: "Duo-Neb contains both albuterol and ipratropium." DuoNeb is a premixed combination: albuterol 2.5 mg + ipratropium 0.5 mg in a single nebulizer vial. When DuoNeb is available, it simplifies administration (one neb setup instead of two). When unavailable, the drugs can be combined in the nebulizer or given separately.

🚫 CONTRAINDICATIONS (1)

Hypersensitivity to the drug, atropine, or its derivatives. The atropine cross-sensitivity is important: because ipratropium is chemically related to atropine, patients with atropine allergy may also react to ipratropium. Ask about atropine allergy before administering.

⚠️ Warnings / Precautions (1)

Narrow angle glaucoma. Anticholinergic drugs can increase intraocular pressure by causing mydriasis (pupil dilation) which blocks aqueous humor drainage. Nebulized ipratropium can contact the eyes, especially with mask delivery. If using in a glaucoma patient, prefer mouthpiece over mask to minimize eye exposure.

🚫 Adverse Reactions (6)

Tachycardia; coughing; tremor; nervousness; nausea; blurred vision.

Tachycardia: anticholinergic effect (blocking vagal brake on heart). Blurred vision: anticholinergic mydriasis, especially if nebulizer mist contacts eyes. Several adverse reactions overlap with albuterol (tachycardia, tremor, nervousness) — when given together as DuoNeb, these effects may be additive.
