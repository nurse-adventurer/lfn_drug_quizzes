Do Not Repeat Dose

LFN 12.78 explicitly states "Do not repeat dose" for both adult and pediatric. If ongoing paralysis is needed after the 8-minute duration, switch to rocuronium (0.5 mg/kg maintenance per LFN 12.74).

Pediatric Dose is a Flat 2 mg/kg — Not a Range

For patients <10 years old, the protocol specifies 2 mg/kg IV as a fixed dose. The adult dose range (1–2 mg/kg) does not apply to this age group.

Pharmacology

Mechanism of Action
Depolarizing NMB agent. Acts similar to ACh, produces depolarization of the motor endplate at the myoneural junction, causing sustained flaccid skeletal muscle paralysis via state of accommodation in adjacent excitable muscle membranes
Pharmacologic Category
Depolarizing neuromuscular blocker agent

Pregnancy

Succinylcholine crosses the placenta. An increased risk of major congenital anomalies has not been observed.

Special Considerations

SC 1 — Drug of Choice for RSI Unless Contraindicated

Succinylcholine is the NMB of choice unless contraindications are present:

  • Pediatric patient <10 yo (rare risk of acute rhabdomyolysis and hyperkalemia)
  • Hyperkalemia
  • Renal failure or dialysis patient
  • Rhabdomyolysis
  • Crush injury (h/o crush injury >24 hours old; scene call — persistent crush >1 hr), severe hypothermia
  • Muscular dystrophy, myopathy
  • Previous recent history of burns

SC 2 — Rocuronium is Paralytic of Choice in Pedi <10 yo

Rocuronium is the paralytic agent of choice in pediatric patients <10 yo — except for emergency intubation or instances where immediate securing of the airway is necessary (e.g., laryngospasm).

If succinylcholine is utilized in patients <10 years old: dose is 2 mg/kg IV.

SC 3 — Antidote for Malignant Hyperthermia

Antidote for malignant hyperthermia is dantrolene.

Warnings, Contraindications & Adverse Reactions

Contraindications

  • Hypersensitivity
  • History of malignant hyperthermia
  • Hyperkalemia
  • Subacute burns
  • Multiple sclerosis
  • ALS (amyotrophic lateral sclerosis)
  • Crush injury
  • Muscular dystrophy
  • Subacute spinal cord injury (less than 6 months)
  • Patient age <10 years, unless emergent airway needed

Warnings / Precautions

May cause hyperkalemia and/or malignant hyperthermia.

Adverse Reactions

Muscular fasciculations — expected depolarizing response

Key Points — LFN 12.78

  • Single indication: Paralysis for RSI
  • Adult: 1–2 mg/kg IV · Max 200 mg · Do not repeat dose
  • Pedi (<10 yr): 2 mg/kg IV flat (not a range) · Max 200 mg · Do not repeat
  • Onset: 30–60 sec / Peak: 3 min / Duration: 8 min
  • Depolarizing mechanism → muscular fasciculations before paralysis (only listed adverse reaction)
  • Trade names: Anectine; Quelicin
  • Drug of choice for RSI unless contraindications present (SC 1)
  • Rocuronium preferred in pedi <10 yo; exception: emergent airway / laryngospasm (SC 2)
  • 10 contraindications — including MH history, hyperkalemia, subacute burns, ALS, MS, muscular dystrophy, SCI <6 months
  • Warning: may cause hyperkalemia and/or malignant hyperthermia
  • MH antidote: dantrolene (SC 3)
