💡 Dual Role — Antiarrhythmic + Local Anesthetic

Lidocaine is unique in the LFN formulary — it serves as both a Class Ib antiarrhythmic (cardiac arrest, tachycardias, post-ROSC) and a local anesthetic (chest tube placement, IO pain). Different indications use different routes, concentrations, and dosing strategies.

📊 Onset / Peak / Duration

Onset: 3 min  |  Peak: 10 min  |  Duration: 2 hours
Moderate onset (3 min) with relatively long duration (2 hrs) supports post-ROSC infusion strategy — bolus during arrest followed by maintenance infusion to prevent recurrence.

🔬 Mechanism of Action

Suppresses automaticity in the His-Purkinje system and by elevating electrical stimulation threshold of ventricle during diastole. This reduces ventricular ectopy and suppresses reentrant circuits responsible for VT/VF. As a local anesthetic, it blocks sodium channels in peripheral nerves, preventing nerve impulse propagation.

⚠️ Adverse Reactions

Drowsiness, dizziness, light-headedness — CNS effects from sodium channel blockade. Higher doses/toxicity: seizures, respiratory depression, cardiovascular collapse.

🤰 Pregnancy Considerations

Doses and indications should follow current ACLS guidelines. Appropriate medications should not be withheld due to concerns of fetal teratogenicity.

  • Dual-use medication: Lidocaine functions as both a Class Ib antiarrhythmic (cardiac arrest, tachycardias) and a local anesthetic (chest tube placement, IO pain management). Know which indication you're using and the corresponding dose/route.
  • Cardiac arrest dosing: Initial 1–1.5 mg/kg, repeat with 0.5–0.75 mg/kg, max 3 doses or 3 mg/kg total. Pediatric: 1 mg/kg IV. If ROSC is obtained after a bolus during resuscitation, initiate a post-ROSC infusion at 1–4 mg/min.
  • IO pain management: 40 mg IO over 120 seconds (2 minutes), then allow to dwell for 60 seconds before flushing. Pediatric <20 kg: 0.5 mg/kg IO (max 40 mg). The dwell time is essential for local anesthetic effect at the IO insertion site.
  • Local anesthesia for chest tube: Inject up to 5 mL of 2% solution, fanning out with the injection to cover a diffuse area. Max dose 4 mg/kg. Peds <25 kg: max 0.2 mL/kg.
  • Metoprolol interaction: Per Metoprolol 12.53 SC #3: beta-blockers may increase the serum concentration of lidocaine. Monitor for toxicity when both medications are used.
  • Recommended infusion mixture: 1 gram / 250 mL = 4 mg/mL concentration. At 1 mg/min = 15 mL/hr; at 4 mg/min = 60 mL/hr.

🚫 CONTRAINDICATIONS

Hypersensitivity to amide-type local anesthetics; supraventricular dysrhythmias; heart block.

⚠️ Caution — High-Risk Populations

Patients with liver or renal disease; CHF; marked hypoxia; respiratory depression; hypovolemia; shock; myasthenia gravis; debilitated patients; the elderly; family history of malignant hyperthermia (fulminant hypermetabolism).

⚠️ Adverse Reactions

Drowsiness, dizziness, light-headedness. These are early CNS toxicity signs. Progressive toxicity: perioral numbness → tinnitus → seizures → respiratory depression → cardiovascular collapse.

⚠️ Drug Interaction — Metoprolol

Per Metoprolol 12.53 SC #3: Beta-blockers may increase the serum concentration of lidocaine. Monitor closely for signs of lidocaine toxicity when co-administered.

🤰 Pregnancy Considerations

Doses and indications should follow current ACLS guidelines. Appropriate medications should not be withheld due to concerns of fetal teratogenicity.

📋 Supraventricular Dysrhythmias — KEY CI

Lidocaine is contraindicated in SVT. It is a ventricular antiarrhythmic only — indicated for VF, VT, and ventricular tachycardias. Do NOT use for atrial fibrillation, atrial flutter, SVT, or other supraventricular rhythms. Use adenosine, diltiazem, or beta-blockers for SVT per LFN protocol.
