🔬 Mechanism of Action — Two Theories

Unclear. Several mechanisms are theorized.

Theory 1 — "Lipid Sink": The emulsion may act as a "lipid sink," surrounding a lipophilic drug molecule and rendering it ineffective. The lipid droplets in the emulsion absorb the lipophilic drug from plasma and tissues, sequestering it away from target receptors.

Theory 2 — Myocardial Energy: The fatty acids from the ILE provide the myocardium with a ready energy source, thereby improving cardiac function. In drug-induced cardiogenic shock, the heart's normal fatty acid metabolism may be impaired — providing exogenous fatty acids restores contractility.

🤰 Pregnancy

Indications for lipid emulsion therapy in pregnant women are the same as in nonpregnant women and should not be withheld when indicated. BB/CCB overdose in pregnancy is immediately life-threatening — the antidote should not be delayed.

💊 BB/CCB OD CONTEXT — When to Use Lipid Emulsion

Lipid emulsion is a rescue therapy for severe/refractory BB or CCB overdose. It's typically used when standard treatments have failed: for BB OD: atropine, glucagon, high-dose insulin (HIET), vasopressors; for CCB OD: calcium chloride/gluconate, high-dose insulin (HIET), vasopressors. Lipid emulsion is added when the patient remains hemodynamically unstable despite these interventions.

💧 WHY LIPOPHILIC DRUGS RESPOND — The "Sink" Concept

Both beta-blockers (especially propranolol, which is highly lipophilic) and calcium channel blockers (especially verapamil, diltiazem) are lipophilic — they dissolve preferentially in fat. The lipid emulsion creates a circulating "fat compartment" that absorbs these drugs out of plasma → reduces free drug at cardiac receptors → restores cardiac function. The more lipophilic the drug, the more effectively the lipid sink works.

⚠️ HIGH VOLUME INFUSION — Practical Challenges

At 0.25-0.5 mL/kg/min for a 70 kg patient, you're running 1050-2100 mL/hr. This creates practical challenges during transport: ensure you have ENOUGH lipid emulsion (multiple 500 mL bags may be needed), monitor for fluid overload signs (especially in CCB OD where cardiac function is already compromised), and consider that this high-volume lipid infusion will interfere with lab values (lipemic serum affects many tests).

🚫 CONTRAINDICATIONS (Multiple)

Impaired ability to metabolize fat: hyperlipidemia, lipoid nephrosis, acute pancreatitis, severe liver damage.

Hypersensitivity to egg, soy, or peanut protein. The emulsion is made with soy oil and egg lecithin — these are structural components, not trace contaminants. Cross-allergic reaction between soybean and peanut has been observed.

⚠️ Warnings / Precautions

Contains soy oil and egg lecithin which may rarely cause allergic reactions. Cross-allergic reaction has been observed between soybean and peanut.

Before giving: ask about egg, soy, and peanut allergies. In a truly life-threatening BB/CCB OD with cardiovascular collapse, the benefit may outweigh allergy risk — clinical judgment required.

🚫 Adverse Reactions (4)

Hypertriglyceridemia; fat embolism; infection; hypersensitivity reactions.

• Hypertriglyceridemia: Expected — you're infusing fat
• Fat embolism: Rare but serious — lipid droplets can occlude small vessels
• Infection: Lipid emulsions support bacterial growth — use aseptic technique
• Hypersensitivity: Egg/soy/peanut components

In emergent BB/CCB OD with hemodynamic collapse: benefits far outweigh risks.
