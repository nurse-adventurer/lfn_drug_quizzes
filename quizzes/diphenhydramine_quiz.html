🔬 Mechanism of Action

Blocks histamine receptors. Histamine causes bronchoconstriction and vasodilation. Blocking the receptors will not reverse these effects but will prevent further incidence. This is the critical MOA distinction: diphenhydramine PREVENTS future histamine effects but does NOT reverse effects already occurring. This is why epinephrine (which actively reverses bronchospasm and hypotension) is the first-line treatment for anaphylaxis, not diphenhydramine.

🤰 Pregnancy

Diphenhydramine crosses the placenta. May be used as adjunctive therapy for nausea/vomiting of pregnancy when preferred agents don't provide initial symptom improvement. Note: pregnant/nursing mothers are listed as a contraindication — weigh risk vs benefit carefully.

  • If administered rapidly via IV, can cause chest tightness, dizziness, anxiety, or hallucinations. Recommended rate of administration is 25 mg/minute. For 50 mg dose: at least 2 minutes. For 25 mg dose: at least 1 minute. The anticholinergic effects are amplified with rapid IV push.
  • Not a first line agent for nausea and/or vomiting. Use ondansetron (12.63) first, then consider promethazine (12.71) or droperidol (12.25) before diphenhydramine. Diphenhydramine is reserved for REFRACTORY nausea after other agents have failed.
🚨 SC #1 — SLOW IV PUSH or Hallucinations

The 25 mg/minute rate limit is specific and testable. Rapid IV push causes a sudden surge of anticholinergic CNS effects: chest tightness, dizziness, anxiety, hallucinations. These can be mistaken for an allergic reaction (chest tightness) or a psychiatric emergency (hallucinations/anxiety). The solution is simple: push slowly. SIVP = Slow IV Push.

⚠️ SC #2 — NOT First-Line for Nausea

LFN anti-emetic hierarchy for nausea/vomiting:
1st: Ondansetron 12.63 (5-HT3 antagonist)
2nd: Promethazine 12.71 (phenothiazine) or Droperidol 12.25
3rd: Lorazepam 12.48 (benzo — for anticipatory/refractory)
Last resort: Diphenhydramine 12.22 (antihistamine — not 1st line)
Per SC #2: explicitly "not a first line agent for nausea and/or vomiting."

🚫 CONTRAINDICATIONS (4)

1. Asthma (anticholinergic: dries bronchial secretions, thickens mucus, worsens airway)
2. COPD (same secretion-drying mechanism worsens COPD)
3. Glaucoma (anticholinergic mydriasis can precipitate acute angle-closure crisis)
4. Pregnant/nursing mothers (crosses placenta, teratogenic concern)

⚠️ Warnings / Precautions

Use with caution in patients with altered mental status. Diphenhydramine causes CNS depression, confusion, and delirium — adding it to an already altered patient can worsen the clinical picture and confound neurological assessment. Alcohol, opioids, and sedatives may result in respiratory depression — additive CNS depression with concurrent depressants.

🚫 Adverse Reactions (7)

Sedation; dries bronchial secretions; blurred vision; headache; palpitations; tachycardia; wheezing; dry mouth. All are anticholinergic effects: "hot as a hare, dry as a bone, blind as a bat, mad as a hatter, red as a beet." Tachycardia occurs from blocking vagal parasympathetic tone. Wheezing (despite being an antihistamine) relates to mucus plugging from dried secretions.
