💡 SELECT Patient Population (SC #1)

Dopamine should typically only be used as a first-line agent in select patient populations — specifically those presenting with bradycardia AND hypotension. For most shock states, norepinephrine is the preferred first-line vasopressor. Dopamine's beta₁ chronotropic effect (↑ HR) is its unique advantage over norepi in bradycardic patients.

🔬 Mechanism of Action — Triple Receptor System

Major cardiovascular effects produced by direct action on alpha-receptors, beta-receptors, and specific dopaminergic receptors in mesenteric and renal vascular beds. Effects are dose-dependent — lower doses favor beta₁/dopaminergic effects while higher doses recruit alpha effects.

🤰 Pregnancy

Should not be withheld in critically ill pregnant patients. May be considered post-resuscitation; effects of vasoactive meds on the fetus should also be considered.

  • First-line in SELECT patients only — those with bradycardia AND hypotension. Not a general first-line vasopressor for most shock states.
  • Do NOT mix with sodium bicarbonate. Chemical incompatibility can inactivate the drug.
  • 2–10 mcg/kg/min = predominantly beta₁ effects → increases cardiac output. This is the dose range that addresses the bradycardic patient's primary problem.
  • >10 mcg/kg/min = predominantly alpha-1 effects → vasoconstriction. Higher doses shift receptor dominance from cardiac stimulation to peripheral vasoconstriction.

🚫 CONTRAINDICATIONS

Hypersensitivity; uncorrected tachyarrhythmias; ventricular fibrillation; pheochromocytoma.

⚠️ Use With Caution

Occlusive vascular disease (Buerger's, Raynaud's), cold injury, diabetic endarteritis, arterial embolism, or patients where increase in HR would be detrimental.

⚠️ Adverse Reactions

Tachycardia; hypertension; widening QRS complex; palpitations. Tachycardia is the most clinically significant — it's the reason dopamine is reserved for bradycardic patients and contraindicated in tachyarrhythmias.

🚫 Pheochromocytoma — Unique CI

Dopamine can trigger hypertensive crisis in pheochromocytoma patients by stimulating catecholamine release from the tumor. This is a unique contraindication not shared with norepinephrine or dobutamine.

📋 Why Not First-Line for Most Shock?

Dopamine increases HR more than norepinephrine, leading to higher arrhythmia risk and increased myocardial oxygen demand. Studies show worse outcomes vs norepinephrine in septic shock. LFN reserves it for the niche where chronotropy is the desired effect: bradycardia + hypotension.
