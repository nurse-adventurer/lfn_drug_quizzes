🤰 Pregnancy

Nicardipine has limited placental transfer following maternal oral and IV administration. Nicardipine may be used as an alternative agent for the treatment of acute-onset, severe hypertension in patients who are pregnant.

⏱️ DURATION — 4–6 Hours (LFN-Specific Value)

LFN 12.59 specifies immediate onset, immediate peak, 4–6 hour duration. Note: onset is immediate (not 5–15 min as in some references). Duration of 4–6 hours means BP effects persist well after stopping the infusion — relevant when titrating to goal then weaning rate.

🫀 Aortic Emergencies — Starting Range 2.5–5 mg/hr

The aortic emergency indication uses a starting range of 2.5–5 mg/hr, not the fixed 5 mg/hr used for stroke indications. This lower starting option reflects that aortic dissection patients may need more conservative BP reduction — the goal is rapid but controlled lowering to reduce shear stress. Once at target, decrease infusion rate to maintain desired BP.

🩸 Hemorrhagic Stroke — Trigger-Based, Narrow Target

Nicardipine for hemorrhagic stroke/cerebral edema is only initiated if SBP >150 mmHg. Target is a narrow window: SBP 140–150 mmHg. Treating below 150 trigger or targeting below 140 risks worsening cerebral ischemia in perihematomal tissue. Labetalol (12.43) is the second-line agent if nicardipine alone cannot control BP.

📋 KEY PROTOCOL RELATIONSHIPS
  • Labetalol (12.43) relationship: For hemorrhagic stroke, LFN 12.43 lists labetalol as indicated "if BP not controlled by Nicardipine alone." Nicardipine is first-line; labetalol is rescue/adjunct.
  • Ischemic stroke non-tPA trigger: SBP >220 OR DBP >120. Goal is 15% reduction — not an absolute BP target. Protect perfusion pressure.
  • Ischemic stroke post-tPA target: 180/105 exactly. Exceeding this risks hemorrhagic transformation; undertreating risks re-bleeding.
  • Mixture prep: Remove 20 mL first, then add two 25 mg vials → 50 mg/230 mL (label says 250 mL; final concentration 0.2 mg/mL per LFN mix table).
  • All indications adults only: No LFN Standing Order for pediatrics on any indication.
🚫 CONTRAINDICATIONS (2)

1. Hypersensitivity to nicardipine or any component.

2. Advanced aortic stenosis — vasodilation in the setting of a fixed outflow obstruction can cause severe hypotension and cardiovascular collapse. Aortic stenosis impairs the compensatory cardiac output increase needed when afterload drops.

⚠️ Warnings / Precautions (2)

1. May worsen heart failure or left ventricular dysfunction — particularly with concomitant beta-blockade. The negative inotropic effect, while minimal at standard doses, can be unmasked in patients with impaired LV function. Combined with a beta blocker, the cardiac depression is additive.

2. Use caution in kidney impairment; increase dose cautiously. Clearance of nicardipine is diminished in renal impairment — titrate more slowly and to lower maximum rates in these patients.

🚫 Adverse Reactions (7)

Bradycardia; hypotension; nausea/vomiting; CHF; Stevens Johnson syndrome; acute renal failure; dysrhythmia.

• Bradycardia — despite being a vasodilatory CCB, bradycardia is listed (distinguish from reflex tachycardia, which is a general CCB concern but not the LFN-listed adverse reaction)
• Hypotension — from excessive vasodilation; overshoot when titrating
• CHF — negative inotropy can precipitate failure in susceptible patients
• Stevens Johnson syndrome — rare serious dermatologic adverse reaction
• Acute renal failure — risk increased in renal impairment (see warnings)
• Dysrhythmia — possible with rapid titration or hemodynamic instability
