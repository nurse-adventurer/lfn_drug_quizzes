Mechanism of Action

Causes an increase in plasma osmolality. Higher sodium concentration causes blood to be hypertonic compared to cerebral tissue, creating an osmotic gradient that promotes flow of excess water from cerebral tissue to blood via osmosis.

Contraindications
Hypo/hypernatremia · Fluid retention · Hypertonic uterus
Adverse Reactions

Hypertension, hematochezia, acute renal failure, acute tubular necrosis, urine discoloration, nausea/vomiting, peripheral edema.

  1. Goal serum sodium is 144–155 mEq/L.
  2. Administer via central line or large bore peripheral IV when possible.
  3. 6 mL/kg of 3% saline will raise serum sodium by 5 mEq/L. 20 mL/kg of NS has the same mEq of sodium as 6 mL/kg of 3%.
  4. Treatment for hyponatremia should be on the order of a physician.
💡 Clinical Context — ICP Management

HTS vs Mannitol: Both create osmotic gradients. HTS stays intravascular and expands plasma volume. Mannitol is an osmotic diuretic that may cause hypotension. HTS may have less rebound ICP elevation.

Repeat dosing: May repeat ×1 PRN. Monitor serum sodium — target 144–155 mEq/L.

🔑 Hyponatremia Key Points

Provider consult required for all hyponatremia treatment.

Mild/moderate (Na⁺ < 120): 0.5–2 mL/kg/hr. Severe (seizures): 3–6 mL/kg/hr.

Pediatric: Use NS for mild/moderate. Reserve 3% for severe symptoms only.

Pregnancy Considerations

May be used in pregnancy. Contraindicated in hypertonic uterus.

🚨 CONTRAINDICATION — Hypo/Hypernatremia

Contraindicated in patients with existing hyponatremia or hypernatremia (context-dependent). Monitor serum sodium closely. Goal range 144–155 mEq/L.

🚨 CONTRAINDICATION — Fluid Retention

Contraindicated in patients with fluid retention. HTS draws water intravascularly, worsening volume overload and pulmonary edema.

🚨 CONTRAINDICATION — Hypertonic Uterus

Contraindicated in patients with a hypertonic uterus.

⚠️ Heart Failure & Hypertension

Caution in patients with heart failure and hypertension. HTS expands intravascular volume, which can precipitate decompensated heart failure.

⚠️ Extravasation Risk

Concentrated NaCl solutions (>1%) are irritants with vesicant-like properties. Ensure proper catheter placement. Avoid extravasation.

⚠️ Osmotic Demyelination Syndrome

Overly rapid correction of hyponatremia can cause ODS. Treatment requires physician order. Use severity-appropriate rates.
