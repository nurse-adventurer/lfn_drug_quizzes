📊 Onset / Peak / Duration

Onset: 5–15 min  |  Peak: Not specified  |  Duration: 3 hours
The 3-hour duration supports LFN's single-dose strategy — no follow-up infusion needed. A single 2 g dose provides antifibrinolytic coverage through transport to the trauma center.

🔬 Mechanism of Action

TXA is a synthetic amino acid that competitively inhibits plasminogen activation. It forms a reversible complex that displaces plasminogen from fibrin, resulting in inhibition of fibrinolysis — preventing breakdown of blood clots that have already formed.

🧬 HOW TXA WORKS — Fibrinolysis Inhibition
Without TXA (Normal Fibrinolysis)
Clot Breakdown
Plasminogen → Plasmin
Plasmin breaks down fibrin
Clots dissolve (fibrinolysis)
→ Continued bleeding in trauma
With TXA (Antifibrinolytic)
Clot Preserved
TXA blocks plasminogen activation
Displaces plasminogen from fibrin
Fibrinolysis inhibited
→ Existing clots stabilized

⚠️ Adverse Reactions

Nausea/vomiting; headache; visual disturbances

🤰 Pregnancy Considerations

TXA crosses the placenta; concentrations within cord blood are similar to maternal serum. A significant reduction in risk of death due to postpartum hemorrhage (PPH) was observed when treatment was started within 3 hours of vaginal birth or cesarean section.

  • Blood products & life threats take priority: Blood administration and the treatment of other life threats must take priority over TXA administration. TXA should be given after blood is initiated (when available) and after all other life threats have been addressed.
  • ≤ 2-hour time window: The injury must have occurred ≤ 2 hours prior to administration. Outside that timeframe requires medical consult or orders from the sending provider.
  • Separate IV line from blood products: TXA may be given concurrently with blood products but must use a separate IV line. Do not administer in the same line as blood products (per 12.82 Warnings and 8.02 Pearl #6).
  • Other agencies may use different dosing: Hospitals and EMS agencies may use a different strategy (e.g., 1 g bolus + 1 g infusion over 8 hours). If that infusion was initiated prior to your arrival, continue it.
  • Inform receiving team: Advise the receiving physician and RN that a total of 2 grams was given. Also advise that no TXA infusion was initiated (total dose given via one-time bolus per LFN protocol).
  • Not approved for neonates: Per 8.02 Blood Product Administration, pediatric TXA (15 mg/kg, max 1 g) is not approved for neonates.

💡 Clinical Context — Why TXA in Trauma?

Severe trauma triggers a hyperfibrinolytic state where clots break down faster than they form, contributing to coagulopathy and uncontrolled hemorrhage. TXA stabilizes existing clots by blocking plasminogen activation. The CRASH-2 trial demonstrated mortality reduction when TXA was given within 3 hours of injury, with the greatest benefit within the first hour. LFN's ≤ 2-hour window aligns with this evidence.

🚫 CONTRAINDICATIONS

Thromboembolic disease (active DVT, PE, or arterial thrombosis); subarachnoid hemorrhage.

🚫 DO NOT Administer in Same Line as Blood Products

TXA should not be administered in the same line as blood products. If giving TXA concurrently with blood, use a separate IV line. This is reinforced in both 12.82 Warnings and 8.02 Blood Product Administration Pearl #6.

⚠️ Hypotension with Rapid Infusion

TXA may cause hypotension with rapid infusion. Must be infused over 10 minutes — do not push rapidly. In already-hypotensive trauma patients, rapid administration could worsen hemodynamic instability.

⚠️ ≤ 2-Hour Time Window

Per LFN guidelines, injury must have occurred ≤ 2 hours prior to administration. Outside that timeframe requires medical consult or sending provider orders. Evidence suggests TXA given > 3 hours after injury may increase mortality risk.

⚠️ Adverse Reactions

Nausea/vomiting; headache; visual disturbances. Monitor for visual changes — retinal changes reported with prolonged use. In acute trauma, these effects are generally mild and self-limiting.

🤰 Pregnancy Considerations

TXA crosses the placenta; cord blood concentrations similar to maternal serum. Significant reduction in PPH death risk when started within 3 hours of vaginal birth or cesarean section. Favorable risk-benefit in obstetric hemorrhage.

👶 Neonatal Restriction

Per 8.02 Blood Product Administration: pediatric TXA is not approved for neonates.
