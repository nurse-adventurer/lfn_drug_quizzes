🔬 Mechanism of Action

Butyrophenone antipsychotic; antiemetic effect is a result of blockade of dopamine stimulation of the chemoreceptor trigger zone. Other effects include alpha-adrenergic blockade, peripheral vascular dilation, and reduction of the pressor effect of epinephrine resulting in hypotension and decreased peripheral vascular resistance; may also reduce pulmonary artery pressure. Three distinct pharmacologic actions: (1) D2 dopamine blockade → antipsychotic + antiemetic, (2) alpha-adrenergic blockade → vasodilation + hypotension, (3) cardiac K+ channel blockade → QT prolongation.

🤰 Pregnancy

Evaluated for adjunctive management of hyperemesis gravidarum; however, use for the treatment of persistent symptoms of nausea and vomiting in pregnancy is not recommended.

  • Reduce dose in the elderly and/or in patients receiving concomitant sedating medications. Elderly patients are more susceptible to QT prolongation, hypotension, and extrapyramidal effects. Concomitant CNS depressants (opioids, benzos, alcohol) amplify respiratory depression and sedation.
  • Dystonic reactions may be treated with diphenhydramine (Benadryl). Dopamine D2 blockade in basal ganglia creates acetylcholine/dopamine imbalance → dystonia (torticollis, oculogyric crisis, trismus). Diphenhydramine's anticholinergic effect restores the balance. Dose: 1 mg/kg SIVP (max 50 mg).
  • NS, D5, or LR may be used to dilute prior to IV administration. Unlike diazepam (which can NEVER be diluted), droperidol is compatible with common IV solutions. This allows for slower, more controlled administration.
🚨 QTc GENDER THRESHOLDS — The Most Critical Safety Check

Per CI: Prolonged QTc is defined as >440 msec in males or >450 msec in females. Obtain a 12-lead ECG before droperidol if possible. If QTc exceeds these thresholds, droperidol is CONTRAINDICATED. Also contraindicated in congenital long QT syndrome. The gender difference reflects normal physiologic QT variation (females have slightly longer baseline QTc).

🚫 CONTRAINDICATIONS (2)

1. Hypersensitivity to droperidol or any formulation component.
2. Known or suspected QT prolongation, including congenital long QT syndrome — prolonged QTc defined as >440 msec in males or >450 msec in females.

⚠️ Warnings / Precautions (Extensive "Extreme Caution" List)

Use extreme caution in: bradycardia (<50 bpm), cardiac disease, concurrent MAO inhibitor therapy, Class I and Class III antiarrhythmics or other drugs known to prolong QT interval, and electrolyte disturbances (hypokalemia or hypomagnesemia), including concomitant drugs which may alter electrolytes (diuretics). Hypokalemia + droperidol = dramatically increased TdP risk.

🚫 Adverse Reactions (20+) — Largest List in Recent Drugs

Cardiac: cardiac arrhythmia (including ventricular), hypotension, prolonged QT, tachycardia, torsades de pointes VT.
CNS: anxiety, chills, depression, dizziness, drowsiness, dysphoria, extrapyramidal reactions (akathisia, dystonia), hallucination, hyperactive behavior, NMS, restlessness, sedated state, shivering, vertigo.
Respiratory: laryngospasm, bronchospasm, respiratory depression.
