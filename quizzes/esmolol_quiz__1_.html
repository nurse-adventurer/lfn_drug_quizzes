🔄 Esmolol Titration Staircase

Load: 500 mcg/kg → Start: 50 mcg/kg/min → 4 min: re-bolus + ↑ to 100 → 4 min: re-bolus + ↑ to 150 → continue ↑ by 50 q 4 min → Max: 300 mcg/kg/min. Repeat the loading dose with each infusion rate increase until target vitals are achieved.

📊 Onset / Peak / Duration

Onset: 2–10 min  |  Peak/Duration: 10–30 min
Ultra-short acting beta blocker — this is the key advantage of esmolol. The short duration means adverse effects (bradycardia, hypotension) resolve quickly if the infusion is stopped. Half-life is approximately 9 minutes.

🔬 Mechanism of Action

Competitively blocks response to beta₁-adrenergic stimulation with little or no effect on beta₂-receptors except at high doses. No intrinsic sympathomimetic activity. No membrane-stabilizing activity. This beta₁-selectivity means esmolol primarily affects heart rate and contractility with minimal bronchospasm risk at standard doses.

⚠️ Adverse Reactions

Bradycardia; hypotension. These are the primary adverse reactions and are extensions of the drug's beta-blocking mechanism. Both resolve rapidly when the infusion is discontinued due to esmolol's ultra-short half-life.

🤰 Pregnancy Considerations

Exposure to esmolol may cause fetal bradycardia which may continue after esmolol is discontinued. Agents other than esmolol may be preferred for the treatment of SVT, atrial fibrillation, atrial flutter, and ventricular tachycardia in pregnancy.

  • Do NOT mix with sodium bicarbonate, diazepam, furosemide, or thiopental. These four medications are incompatible with esmolol and will precipitate or degrade the drug.
  • Recommended mixture: 2,500 mg / 250 mL (10 mg/mL concentration). Use the dosing chart for weight-based calculations at each infusion tier.
  • Repeat the loading dose (500 mcg/kg) with each infusion rate increase. The re-bolus is necessary because esmolol's ultra-short half-life means the infusion alone takes time to reach new steady state — the bolus bridges the gap.
  • Adult only — no pediatric LFN standing order. Esmolol dosing in the LFN formulary applies exclusively to adult patients.
  • Titrate to target vitals. The stepwise 50 mcg/kg/min titration every 4 minutes allows precise heart rate and blood pressure control. Continuously monitor hemodynamics during each titration step.

🚫 CONTRAINDICATIONS

Decompensated heart failure; cardiogenic shock; 2nd or 3rd degree heart blocks (without artificial pacemaker); bradycardia; recent administration of calcium channel blocker.

⚠️ Hyperkalemia Risk

Esmolol is associated with elevations in serum potassium and development of hyperkalemia. Monitor potassium levels, especially in patients with renal impairment or those on potassium-sparing medications.

⚠️ Hypotension

Hypotension is both an adverse reaction and a warning. Esmolol reduces cardiac output → can precipitate or worsen hypotension. Monitor BP closely with each titration step.

⚠️ PVD / Raynaud Disease

Can precipitate or aggravate symptoms of arterial insufficiency in patients with peripheral vascular disease (PVD) and Raynaud disease. Beta-blockade reduces peripheral perfusion.

🚫 Drug Incompatibilities

Do NOT mix esmolol with: sodium bicarbonate, diazepam, furosemide, or thiopental. These four drugs are physically/chemically incompatible with esmolol solutions.

🤰 Pregnancy Considerations

May cause fetal bradycardia that persists after discontinuation. Other agents are preferred for SVT, AFib/AFlutter, and VT in pregnancy.

📋 Adverse Reactions

Bradycardia and hypotension are the primary adverse effects. Due to esmolol's ultra-short half-life (~9 min), these effects resolve rapidly when the infusion is stopped or reduced — this is the key safety advantage over longer-acting beta blockers.
