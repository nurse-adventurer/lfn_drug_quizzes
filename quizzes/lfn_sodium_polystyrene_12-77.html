📝 Special Considerations

No LFN Special Considerations Listed

LFN 12.77 does not list numbered Special Considerations for this medication. All clinical guidance is contained in Warnings/Precautions, Contraindications, and the Pregnancy section.

⏱️ Slow Onset — Clinical Context

Onset is hours; peak 4–6 hours. This drug removes potassium from the body via the GI tract. It is not absorbed systemically. For acute hyperkalemia (peaked T waves, wide QRS, cardiac arrest), use faster-acting agents first: calcium (membrane stabilization), insulin/glucose or sodium bicarbonate (K⁺ shift into cells). Kayexalate provides ongoing K⁺ removal after stabilization.

🤰 Pregnancy Safety

Not absorbed systemically following oral or rectal administration. Use during pregnancy is not expected to result in significant exposure to the fetus.

⚠️ Warnings, Contraindications & Adverse Reactions

🚫 Contraindications (4)

  • Hypersensitivity to sodium polystyrene sulfonate, polystyrene sulfonate resins, or any component of the formulation
  • Obstructive bowel disease
  • Neonates with reduced gut motility
  • Oral administration in neonates

⚠️ Warnings / Precautions (3)

  • Severe hypokalemia may occur
  • Large oral doses may cause fecal impaction
  • Use with caution in patients with severe heart failure and/or hypertension — sodium load may exacerbate condition

⚠️ Adverse Reactions

Hypernatremia, hypocalcemia, hypokalemia, hypomagnesemia, sodium retention, constipation, diarrhea, fecal impaction, nausea, vomiting
