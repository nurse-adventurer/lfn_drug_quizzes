💡 Sodium Equivalence — NS Substitution

6 mL/kg of 3% hypertonic saline will raise serum sodium by ~5 mEq/L. If 3% is not available, 20 mL/kg of normal saline has the same mEq of sodium and may be utilized as a substitute.

Rapid enough for acute ICP emergencies — 15-minute infusion window aligns with onset timing. Effects persist for hours, buying time during transport.

🔬 Mechanism of Action

Causes an increase in plasma osmolality. The higher sodium concentration causes blood to be hypertonic compared to cerebral tissue, which has a lower sodium concentration. This creates an osmotic gradient that promotes the flow of excess water from cerebral tissue to the blood via osmosis, reducing cerebral edema and intracranial pressure.

⚠️ Adverse Reactions

Hypertension, hematochezia, acute renal failure, acute tubular necrosis, urine discoloration, nausea/vomiting, peripheral edema.

🤰 Pregnancy Considerations

May be used in pregnancy.

  • Goal serum sodium 144–155 mEq/L. This is the target range for ICP management with hypertonic saline. Monitor sodium levels closely to avoid overcorrection.
  • Administer via central line or large bore peripheral IV when possible. Concentrations >2% should ideally be given via central line per LFN 12.77 SC #2. In emergencies, a large bore peripheral IV is acceptable.
  • Sodium equivalence: 6 mL/kg of 3% hypertonic saline will raise serum sodium by approximately 5 mEq/L. If 3% is not available, 20 mL/kg of normal saline has the same mEq of sodium and may be used as a substitute.
  • Treatment for hyponatremia should be on the order of a physician. Provider consult is required for hyponatremia treatment — this is NOT a standing order indication for hyponatremia.

🚫 CONTRAINDICATIONS

Hypo/hypernatremia; fluid retention; hypertonic uterus.

⚠️ Caution — High-Risk Populations

Use with caution in patients with heart failure and hypertension. The sodium load can exacerbate fluid overload and worsen blood pressure.

⚠️ Adverse Reactions

Hypertension, hematochezia, acute renal failure, acute tubular necrosis, urine discoloration, nausea/vomiting, peripheral edema. Monitor renal function and hemodynamics closely during administration.

📋 Administration Route — Central Line Preferred

Administer via central line or large bore peripheral IV when possible. Solutions >2% are irritants with vesicant-like properties. Ensure proper needle/catheter placement. Avoid extravasation — can cause tissue injury.

🤰 Pregnancy Considerations

May be used in pregnancy.

📋 Hyponatremia — Physician Order Required

Treatment for hyponatremia should be on the order of a physician. This is NOT a standing order indication. Provider consult is required before initiating hypertonic saline for hyponatremia. Pediatric mild/moderate hyponatremia should be treated with NS first, not HTS.
