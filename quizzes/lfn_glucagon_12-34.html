🔬 Mechanism of Action

Induces liver glycogen breakdown and glucose release; smooth GI muscle relaxant. For BB/CCB overdose, the critical cardiac mechanism is: glucagon directly activates adenylate cyclase, bypassing both beta receptors (blocked by BB) and calcium channels (blocked by CCB). This increases intracellular cAMP → enhanced cardiac contractility (positive inotropy) and heart rate (positive chronotropy). Glucagon works because it uses a DIFFERENT pathway than the blocked receptors.

🫀 WHY GLUCAGON WORKS IN BB/CCB OD — The Bypass Pathway

Normal cardiac stimulation: Catecholamines → beta receptor → adenylate cyclase → cAMP → ↑ HR + contractility
In BB overdose: Beta receptors BLOCKED → catecholamines can't activate adenylate cyclase
Glucagon bypass: Glucagon receptor → directly activates adenylate cyclase → cAMP → ↑ HR + contractility

The glucagon receptor is a SEPARATE receptor from the beta receptor. Beta blockers can't block it. This is why glucagon "works when beta blockers are present" — it uses a completely different door to the same room (cAMP).

🤜 Pregnancy

Glucagon may be used for the treatment of severe hypoglycemia during pregnancy. For BB/CCB overdose in pregnancy, the benefit of treating hemodynamic instability outweighs the risks.

1Ineffective in patients with depleted glycogen stores. Glucagon's metabolic effect (glucose release) depends on intact liver glycogen. In chronic alcoholics, malnourished patients, prolonged fasting, or starvation — glycogen is depleted and glucagon cannot release glucose. The cardiac effects (inotropy/chronotropy via cAMP) may still partially work, but the metabolic rescue fails. Use dextrose instead.
2IV dextrose should be administered as soon as possible. Glucagon depletes remaining glycogen stores. After glucagon's effect wanes (15 min), the patient is at risk for rebound hypoglycemia because their glycogen is now MORE depleted. Dextrose (12.19) provides exogenous glucose independent of glycogen stores.
3May reduce the effect of beta blockers. This IS the therapeutic goal in BB overdose — glucagon's cAMP-increasing effect counteracts the beta-blocker's cAMP-decreasing effect. SC #3 is listed as a "drug interaction" but it's actually the desired mechanism of action in this context.
⏱️ 15-MINUTE DURATION — The Critical Limitation

Glucagon's duration is only 15 minutes. After 15 minutes, the patient can relapse back into hemodynamic instability. Plan for: (1) repeat dosing if needed, (2) IV dextrose for rebound hypoglycemia per SC #2, (3) definitive treatment (high-dose insulin/glucose therapy, vasopressors, or Intralipid for severe BB/CCB overdose). Glucagon is a bridge, not definitive treatment.

📋 CROSS-REFERENCE: Dextrose 12.19 in BB OD

Dextrose 12.19 specifically lists "Beta Blocker and Salicylate OD w/hypoglycemia" as an indication. When glucagon depletes glycogen (SC #1-2), dextrose provides direct glucose. Also note: beta blockers mask hypoglycemia symptoms (tachycardia, tremor) — the patient may be hypoglycemic without typical warning signs. Check BGL frequently.

🚫 CONTRAINDICATIONS (4) — Unique Endocrine Tumor CIs

1. Hypersensitivity to drug/class/compound.
2. Insulinoma — glucagon stimulates insulin release from insulinoma → paradoxical severe hypoglycemia (opposite of desired effect).
3. Pheochromocytoma — glucagon triggers catecholamine release from adrenal tumor → hypertensive crisis.
4. Glucagonoma (diagnostic use) — already producing excess glucagon; exogenous glucagon is unnecessary and may worsen hyperglycemia/symptoms.

⚠️ Warnings / Precautions — "Caution If" List

Caution if: prolonged fasting, starvation, adrenal insufficiency, chronic hypoglycemia, cardiac disease (diagnostic use), diabetes (diagnostic use). The prolonged fasting/starvation caution connects to SC #1 (depleted glycogen stores). Adrenal insufficiency patients may have blunted counter-regulatory responses.

🚫 Adverse Reactions (1 — But Important)

Nausea and vomiting. Extremely common at the high toxicology doses (5-10 mg). Glucagon relaxes GI smooth muscle AND stimulates the chemoreceptor trigger zone. Have suction ready — especially important in obtunded patients at risk for aspiration. The N/V may be worse than the drug's other effects at these high doses.
