🔬 Mechanism of Action

An essential element in many biochemical processes. Acts as a physiologic calcium channel blocker and blocks neuromuscular transmission.

This dual mechanism explains ALL 4 indications:
• Torsades: Membrane stabilization → suppresses EADs (early afterdepolarizations)
• Asthma: Ca²⁺ antagonism → bronchial smooth muscle relaxation
• Tocolysis: Ca²⁺ antagonism → uterine smooth muscle relaxation
• Eclampsia: NMDA antagonism + Ca²⁺ blockade → ↓ neuronal excitability → ↓ seizure threshold

🤰 Pregnancy

Magnesium crosses the placenta; fetal serum concentrations are similar to maternal. Used for prevention and treatment of seizures in pregnant/postpartum patients with eclampsia or preeclampsia with severe features. Two of the four indications ARE pregnancy-specific (preterm labor + eclampsia).

☠️ TOXICITY PROGRESSION — The Critical Monitoring Sequence

DTR loss → Respiratory depression → Cardiac arrest. This predictable sequence is why DTR monitoring is the MOST important bedside assessment during magnesium infusion.

• 4-7 mEq/L: Therapeutic (flushing, warmth — expected)
• 7-10 mEq/L: Loss of DTRs, weakness, drowsiness (STOP infusion)
• 10-15 mEq/L: Respiratory paralysis, hypotension
• >15 mEq/L: Complete heart block, cardiac arrest

Antidote: Calcium gluconate 1 g over 3-5 min (12.12), may repeat PRN.

📊 SC #2 — THERAPEUTIC LEVEL: 4-7 mEq/L for PIH and Pre-term Labor

The therapeutic range is narrow: 4-7 mEq/L. DTR loss begins at ~7 mEq/L — meaning there's essentially NO gap between "therapeutic" and "first sign of toxicity." This is why SC #5 mandates monitoring DTRs, LOC, and vitals q 5 min × 3, then q 15 min when initiating tocolysis.

📋 SC #1 — TOCOLYSIS VIA IV PUMP

For tocolysis, the initial bolus and infusion should be given via IV pump. The 6 g loading dose over 30 min = 300 mL/hr (using 4 g/100 mL mixture). Maintenance 2 g/hr = 50 mL/hr. Gravity infusion is too imprecise for this narrow therapeutic window.

⚡ ECLAMPSIA ESCALATION — Persistent Seizures Protocol

If eclampsia seizures persist despite the 6 g loading dose: administer an additional 2 grams over 2 minutes AND increase maintenance infusion by 1 gram/hour. This is the ONLY magnesium indication with a rapid 2-minute push — reflecting the life-threatening urgency of persistent eclamptic seizures.

🚫 CONTRAINDICATIONS (6)

Myocardial damage; heart block; shock; persistent hypertension; hypocalcemia; renal failure.

• Heart block: Mg slows conduction → worsens block
• Shock: Mg causes vasodilation → worsens hypotension
• Persistent HTN: Seemingly paradoxical — but Mg doesn't effectively lower BP; the CI may refer to Mg masking HTN severity
• Hypocalcemia: Mg antagonizes Ca²⁺ → worsens deficiency
• Renal failure: Mg excreted renally → accumulation → toxicity

⚠️ Warnings / Precautions (3)

Impaired renal function; digitalized patients; concomitant CNS depressants or neuromuscular blocking agents.

• Renal impairment: Reduce maintenance dose, monitor more frequently
• Digitalis: Mg + digoxin → enhanced heart block risk
• NMB agents (cisatracurium/vecuronium): Mg potentiates NMB → prolonged paralysis. Critical for intubated patients on both Mg and paralytics.

🚫 Adverse Reactions (11)

Sedation; confusion; depressed/absent reflexes; muscle weakness; flaccid paralysis; hypotension; depressed cardiac function; complete heart block; circulatory collapse; respiratory paralysis; flushing; nausea.

These are essentially a spectrum of toxicity from mild (flushing/sedation) through severe (respiratory paralysis → cardiac arrest). ALL are dose-dependent and reflect Mg's Ca²⁺ antagonism and NMJ blockade.
