🚫 Adult Asthma/Croup: No LFN Standing Order

Per LFN 12.73 dose table: adult column for Asthma/Croup reads "No LFN standing orders." This is not a warning or a precaution — it is the absence of a standing order. LFN racemic epinephrine has no adult indication for asthma or croup within the formulary.

Adult racemic epinephrine is only ordered for anaphylaxis (with stridor/upper airway swelling). The peds column has standing orders for both indications.

🔍 Anaphylaxis Qualifier: "For stridor/upper airway swelling"

The adult anaphylaxis dose entry includes the qualifier: "For stridor/upper airway swelling." This limits the racemic epinephrine indication to the airway/upper airway component of anaphylaxis — not systemic anaphylaxis in general. Systemic anaphylaxis is treated with IM epinephrine 0.3 mg (1 mg/mL concentration) per LFN 12.26. Racemic epinephrine targets the mucosal edema and stridor component via inhaled route.

The peds dose for anaphylaxis is listed as "Same as Adult" — 0.5 mL of 2.25% in 2.5 mL NS — identical volume and concentration regardless of patient weight.

🔬 Mechanism of Action (LFN verbatim)

"Stimulates alpha-, beta1-, and beta2-adrenergic receptors resulting in relaxation of smooth muscle of the bronchial tree, cardiac stimulation (increasing myocardial oxygen consumption), and dilation of skeletal muscle vasculature; small doses can cause vasodilation via beta2-vascular receptors; large doses may produce constriction of skeletal and vascular smooth muscle"

📝 MOA in Clinical Context — Three Receptor Effects

LFN 12.73 MOA describes three distinct effect pathways:

1. Bronchial smooth muscle (beta2): Relaxation of bronchial tree smooth muscle → bronchodilation. This is the therapeutic target for asthma and croup (subglottic edema relief).

2. Cardiac stimulation (beta1): Cardiac stimulation increasing myocardial oxygen consumption — a side effect, not a desired action for these indications.

3. Vascular effects (dose-dependent): Small doses → vasodilation via beta2-vascular receptors. Large doses → constriction of skeletal and vascular smooth muscle (alpha predominance). For inhaled racemic epinephrine at 0.5 mL, the local mucosal alpha effects produce vasoconstriction that reduces airway edema — which is the mechanism for relieving croup stridor and anaphylactic upper airway swelling.

Category is "nonselective alpha and beta agonist, bronchodilator" — the bronchodilator label reflects the primary therapeutic use, while the nonselective designation reflects the full receptor profile.

🤰 Pregnancy Considerations

"Epinephrine crosses the placenta. Routine use is not recommended. However, anaphylaxis and asthma exacerbations may have a greater fetal/maternal risk than what is associated with epinephrine."

The LFN 12.73 pregnancy entry uses an indication-risk framework: the drug risk (placental transfer) is weighed against the disease risk (anaphylaxis/asthma exacerbation carries greater fetal/maternal risk than the drug itself). This justifies use when indicated. The phrase "routine use is not recommended" establishes baseline caution while the second sentence permits use for acute emergencies. Compare to the systemic epinephrine 12.26 pregnancy language, which similarly endorses use for anaphylaxis in pregnant women.

📋 Special Considerations

LFN 12.73 has no special considerations listed. This is one of the fewer LFN formulary entries without a SC section. All dosing guidance, including diluent and repeat interval, is contained in the dose table.

🔍 Racemic vs Standard Epinephrine — What LFN 12.73 Uses

"Racemic" means the drug is a 50/50 mixture of the L- and D-isomers of epinephrine (L-epinephrine being the pharmacologically active form used in 12.26 systemic epinephrine). The racemic formulation is used exclusively via nebulization. It is never given IV, IM, or IV push.

The dose is expressed as a fixed volume/concentration (0.5 mL of 2.25% solution) rather than weight-based mcg/kg. The peds dose is explicitly "Same as Adult" — no weight adjustment for pediatric patients.

The 2.25% concentration and 0.5 mL volume are fixed — there is no titration or escalation described in LFN 12.73. The only adjustment is repeating q 20 min PRN.

🚫 CONTRAINDICATION (1)

Hypersensitivity — to racemic epinephrine or any component.

Single CI. No other absolute contraindications listed. This contrasts with the warnings list, which is lengthy — reflecting that the drug can be used in many patients with caution, but only a true hypersensitivity constitutes an absolute CI.

⚠️ Warnings / Precautions (9 conditions)

Caution in:

1. Cardiac disease
2. HTN (hypertension)
3. Hyperthyroidism
4. Diabetes mellitus
5. Glaucoma, angle-closure
6. BPH (benign prostatic hyperplasia)
7. Psychiatric disorder
8. Seizure disorder

Eight distinct conditions listed. Notably, glaucoma/angle-closure are listed as a combined entry. BPH is unusual for a respiratory drug — reflects alpha-adrenergic effects on the urogenital system. The cardiac disease and HTN warnings align with the beta1 cardiac stimulation and dose-dependent vasoconstriction described in the MOA.
