⏱️ DURATION — 45 MIN (LFN-Specific Value)

LFN 12.58 specifies 45 minutes duration — many resources cite 30–90 min. The 45-min value is the LFN protocol figure. Because most opioids last hours (morphine 2–4 hr, methadone 24+ hr), renarcotization is a real risk after naloxone wears off. Monitor closely after administration.

🤰 Pregnancy

Naloxone crosses the placenta. Although naloxone may precipitate opioid withdrawal in the fetus in addition to the mother, treatment should not be withheld when needed in cases of maternal opioid overdose. When using the injection, starting at the low end of the dosing range is suggested to help avoid adverse fetal events but still provide treatment to the mother.

🎯 Dosing Goal — Respiratory Function, Not Consciousness

Per LFN 12.58 warning: "Use the smallest dose possible to improve respiratory function." The goal of naloxone is restoring adequate ventilation (RR >12, adequate tidal volume) — NOT full alertness. Over-reversal precipitates withdrawal, acute pain, combativeness, and in opioid-dependent patients may cause seizures. Titrate to breathing, not wakefulness.

⏱️ Repeat Intervals — Opioid OD vs Clonidine OD Differ

Altered mental status / Opioid OD: Repeat q 3–5 min PRN.
Clonidine OD: Repeat q 1–2 min PRN.

The faster clonidine repeat interval reflects the different pharmacodynamics of clonidine (alpha-2 agonist, not opioid receptor-mediated). Larger doses may also be needed — naïve adults use weight-based dosing (0.1 mg/kg).

📋 KEY CLINICAL PRINCIPLES
  • Renarcotization: Naloxone's 45-min duration is shorter than most opioids. Patient may re-sedate when naloxone wears off. Continuous monitoring mandatory.
  • Combativeness warning: Per LFN 12.58 — "May cause the patient to become combative after administration." Anticipated and documented as a warning, not a contraindication.
  • Opioid-dependent patients: Methadone patients have a specific CI (mother or infant on methadone — seizure risk). Dependent adults have reduced clonidine OD dosing (0.5–1 mg) vs naïve adults (0.1 mg/kg).
  • No max for standard opioid OD (non-clonidine): Adult max is 10 mg total. Repeat 0.4 mg q 3–5 min until respiratory function improves.
  • Routes equivalent for reversal: IV, IM, and IN all listed for both indications. No route restriction in LFN 12.58. IN is not separately dosed — it uses the same 0.4 mg dose.
🚫 CONTRAINDICATIONS (2)

1. Hypersensitivity to naloxone or any component.

2. Mother or infant history of opioid addiction or taking methadone — "as it may precipitate seizures." This CI specifically applies to the maternal-fetal context. A newborn whose mother is on methadone or has opioid addiction should NOT receive naloxone routinely because acute opioid withdrawal in the neonate may precipitate seizures.

⚠️ Warnings / Precautions (2)

1. May cause the patient to become combative after administration. Rapid reversal of opioid-induced CNS depression restores alertness — combined with pain, withdrawal, and disorientation, this predictably causes agitation. Anticipate combativeness and ensure scene/crew safety.

2. Use the smallest dose possible to improve respiratory function. Titrate to breathing, not consciousness. Over-reversal precipitates acute withdrawal syndrome and may cause seizures in opioid-dependent/methadone patients.
