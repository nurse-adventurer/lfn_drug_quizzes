⚠️ PRE-TERM LABOR — DOSE MATH

10 mg q 15 min × 4 doses = 40 mg max total. Once the max dose is reached, no additional nifedipine should be given. The hold criteria (SBP <90) apply before each dose — reassess BP at every 15-minute interval.

🤰 Pregnancy Considerations

Nifedipine crosses the placenta. Oral nifedipine is recommended for the management of acute-onset, severe hypertension in pregnant and postpartum patients, including those with preeclampsia or eclampsia.

Note: the hypertension-in-pregnancy use (preeclampsia/eclampsia) is described in the pregnancy note but is not listed as a separate LFN indication. The two standing order indications are HAPE and pre-term labor. Labetalol (12.43) carries the preeclampsia/eclampsia indication in the LFN formulary.

🔑 CATEGORY: "ANTIANGINALS; CALCIUM CHANNEL BLOCKER"

Unlike nicardipine (12.59) which is categorized as "CCB – dihydropyridine; antihypertensive," nifedipine is classified under antianginals as the primary category, with calcium channel blocker as secondary. LFN 12.60 does not list a hypertension indication — the drug's antihypertensive properties are used clinically (HAPE and tocolysis both involve vascular mechanisms) but the primary category reflects its broader classification.

🏔️ HAPE Mechanism — Why a CCB?

High altitude pulmonary edema results from hypoxic pulmonary vasoconstriction (HPV) — the normal physiologic response to low oxygen at altitude causes exaggerated vasoconstriction in the pulmonary vasculature, raising pulmonary artery pressure and driving fluid into the alveoli. Nifedipine blocks calcium-mediated smooth muscle contraction → reduces pulmonary vascular resistance → ↓ pulmonary artery pressure → reduces fluid transudation. The 20 mg PO dose is the LFN standing order; descent remains the definitive treatment.

🤰 Pre-term Labor — Tocolysis Mechanism

Uterine smooth muscle contraction depends on intracellular calcium. Nifedipine blocks calcium influx → reduces myometrial contractility → inhibits or delays labor. The LFN protocol doses 10 mg PO/SL q 15 min up to 40 mg total. Hold if SBP <90 mmHg — hypotension reduces uteroplacental perfusion and is dangerous in the obstetric patient.

  • No repeat/max stated for HAPE: LFN 12.60 lists 20 mg PO for HAPE with no repeat interval or maximum. Contrast with pre-term labor which has explicit repeat (q 15 min) and max (40 mg).
  • Routes differ between indications: HAPE = PO only. Pre-term labor = PO or SL. Sublingual absorption may be faster, relevant in the acute tocolysis context.
  • Metoprolol interaction (12.53 SC #4): Per LFN 12.53 Special Considerations, metoprolol "may enhance hypotensive effects of... nifedipine." A patient on metoprolol receiving nifedipine is at elevated risk for significant hypotension — especially relevant in the hold criteria (SBP <90).
  • Pregnancy note ≠ indication: Nifedipine is noted as "recommended" for preeclampsia/eclampsia in the pregnancy field but this is not a listed LFN standing order indication. Labetalol (12.43) and magnesium sulfate (12.49) carry the eclampsia/preeclampsia indications in LFN.
  • Aortic stenosis in warnings vs CI: For nifedipine, aortic stenosis appears in Warnings/Precautions — not as a CI. Compare nicardipine (12.59) where "advanced aortic stenosis" is a contraindication. The distinction matters for clinical decision-making.
🚫 CONTRAINDICATIONS (1)

Hypersensitivity to nifedipine or any component. This is the only listed contraindication — notably, aortic stenosis and CHF appear in warnings, not here.

⚠️ Warnings / Precautions (6)

LFN 12.60 lists six warning conditions:

Recent Beta Blocker Use
Enhanced hypotension (see metoprolol SC #4)
Elderly Patients
Increased sensitivity; greater hypotension risk
Hepatic Impairment
Reduced metabolism → increased drug levels
CHF
Negative inotropic effect; ↓ cardiac output
Aortic Stenosis
WARNING (not CI) — unlike nicardipine
Hypotension
Pre-existing ↓ BP; hold for SBP <90 in labor

Pulmonary edema as an adverse reaction is clinically counterintuitive — nifedipine is used to treat HAPE, yet pulmonary edema appears in its adverse reaction list. This reflects the general CCB adverse profile; excessive vasodilation and reflex tachycardia can precipitate pulmonary edema in susceptible patients (especially those with CHF, a listed warning).
