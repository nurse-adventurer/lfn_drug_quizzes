🔬 Mechanism of Action

Exerts minimal effects on beta-1 and alpha receptors. Inhibits histamine release from mast cells, reduces mucus secretion, capillary leaking, and mucosal edema caused by allergic reactions. Beta-2 stimulation → ↑ cAMP → bronchial smooth muscle relaxation → bronchodilation. Also drives K⁺ into cells via Na⁺/K⁺-ATPase activation (hyperkalemia mechanism).

🤰 Pregnancy

Albuterol is the preferred short-acting beta-2 agonist (SABA) for the management of asthma during pregnancy. Untreated maternal hypoxia poses far greater fetal risk than the medication. Inhaled route = minimal systemic absorption.

  • May be given via mouthpiece, mask, or ETT. For intubated patients, in-line nebulizer connected to the ventilator circuit. For non-intubated patients, mouthpiece preferred for better delivery; mask for those who can't hold mouthpiece.
  • Duo-Neb contains both albuterol and ipratropium. Convenient combination product: albuterol 2.5 mg + ipratropium 0.5 mg in one vial. Used for first 2 doses in asthma/COPD per PCG 5.04.

⚡ HYPERKALEMIA — Why a Bronchodilator Treats High K⁺

Beta-2 stimulation activates the Na⁺/K⁺-ATPase pump on skeletal muscle cells, driving potassium FROM blood INTO cells. This temporarily ↓ serum K⁺ by 0.5–1.0 mEq/L. Does NOT remove K⁺ from the body — just shifts it intracellularly. LFN dose: 2.5 mg q 15 min × 4 (max 4 doses). Additive with insulin/D10. The same mechanism causes hypokalemia as an adverse effect.

📋 Contraindications

Hypersensitivity. That's it — albuterol has NO additional contraindications per LFN.

📋 Warnings / Precautions

None listed per LFN 12.05. This is notable — albuterol is one of the safest drugs in the formulary with no specific warnings or precautions listed.

⚠️ Adverse Reactions (6)

Tachycardia; hypokalemia; dysrhythmias; peripheral vasodilation; nervousness; nausea/vomiting. Tachycardia is most common (beta-1 crossover). Hypokalemia is most clinically significant — especially with repeated dosing + concurrent diuretics.

🚫 Hypokalemia — The Hidden Danger of Repeated Dosing

Each dose shifts K⁺ into cells via Na⁺/K⁺-ATPase. With aggressive dosing (continuous nebs, multiple back-to-back treatments), serum K⁺ can drop significantly. Combine with diuretics (furosemide, HCTZ) that cause renal K⁺ loss → additive hypokalemia → dysrhythmias (PVCs, VT, VF). The same drug used to TREAT hyperkalemia can CAUSE hypokalemia as a side effect.

⚠️ "Silent Chest" — When Wheezing Stops But Patient Worsens

A quiet/silent chest in severe respiratory distress is a pre-arrest warning sign. Wheezing requires airflow; when obstruction is so severe that minimal air moves, wheezing disappears. This is NOT improvement — it indicates imminent respiratory failure. Prepare for intubation, escalate to IM epinephrine + IV MgSO₄.
