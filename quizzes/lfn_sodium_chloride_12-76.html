📝 Special Considerations

SC 1 — Multiple Concentrations Exist

There are multiple concentrations and preparations of sodium chloride. Verify the correct preparation before administration.

SC 2 — Central vs Peripheral for Solutions >2%

Solutions that are >2% should ideally be administered via central line; however, in emergency situations, you may utilize a peripheral venous line.

SC 3 — Normal Saline (0.9%)

Most commonly administered sodium chloride preparation.
a. Each liter contains 9 grams (154 mEq) of sodium
b. pH of 5.5

SC 4 — Hypertonic Saline (3%)

Hyperosmolar; typically used to treat head injuries and cerebral edema.
a. Each liter contains 30 grams (513 mEq) of sodium
b. pH of 5

⚠️ Warnings, Contraindications & Adverse Reactions

🚫 Contraindication (1)

  • Hypersensitivity to sodium chloride or any component of the formulation

⚠️ Warning / Precaution — Extravasation Risk

Concentrated solutions of IV sodium chloride (>1%) are irritants (with vesicant-like properties at higher osmolarities); depends on osmolarity; higher osmolarity is associated with a higher risk. Ensure proper needle or catheter placement prior to and during infusion. Avoid extravasation.

⚠️ Adverse Reactions

  • Acid-base imbalance (including hyperchloremic metabolic acidosis, metabolic acidosis)
  • Electrolyte disorder (including hyperchloremia, hyperkalemia)
  • Hypernatremia
  • Hyponatremia

🤰 Pregnancy Considerations

May be used in pregnancy.

🔑 Key Points — LFN 12.76

  • Single indication: Fluid Resuscitation — dose titrates to desired effect per patient presentation
  • Adult typical bolus: 250–500 mL
  • Pediatric typical bolus: 10–20 mL (flat volume per LFN 12.76 wording — not mL/kg)
  • Normal Saline 0.9%: 154 mEq/L Na · pH 5.5 — most commonly administered preparation (SC 3)
  • Hypertonic 3%: 513 mEq/L Na · pH 5 — head injuries / cerebral edema (SC 4)
  • Solutions >2%: central line preferred; peripheral acceptable in emergency (SC 2)
  • Concentrated solutions >1%: irritants with vesicant-like properties — avoid extravasation
  • Adverse effect risk: hyperchloremic metabolic acidosis with large NS volumes (high Cl⁻ load)
  • Contraindication: hypersensitivity (single CI)
  • Pregnancy: may be used
