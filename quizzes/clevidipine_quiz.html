🔬 Mechanism of Action

Inhibits calcium ion influx into vascular smooth muscle and myocardium. As a dihydropyridine CCB, clevidipine is primarily vascular-selective — it relaxes arterial smooth muscle \u2192 \u2193 SVR \u2192 \u2193 BP. Minimal effect on venous capacitance or cardiac conduction (unlike diltiazem/verapamil). Metabolized by blood and tissue esterases — NOT dependent on liver or kidney function.

🩸 LIPID EMULSION FORMULATION — Like Propofol

Clevidipine is formulated in a 20% lipid emulsion containing egg phospholipids and soybean oil (similar to propofol). This is why egg, soy, and lipid metabolism disorders are contraindicated. The lipid emulsion also means the drug should not hang for more than 12 hours once opened (infection risk), and vials should not be used if discolored or particulate.

🤰 Pregnancy

Chronic maternal hypertension is associated with adverse fetal events including birth defects, low birth weight, premature delivery, stillbirth, and neonatal death. Untreated hypertension also risks maternal outcomes including preeclampsia, stroke, and MI. Clevidipine is not specifically recommended or contraindicated in pregnancy per LFN — but labetalol and hydralazine are typically preferred for OB hypertension.

📋 No Numbered Special Considerations in LFN 12.15

Clevidipine has no SCs listed. Key clinical application notes are built into the dosing table and warnings. Important clinical context:

📈 TITRATION PROTOCOL — The "Dial-a-BP" Drug

Clevidipine's rapid onset (2–4 min) and rapid offset (5–15 min after d/c) make it uniquely titratable among IV antihypertensives. The q 2 min titration interval means you can reach target BP within 10–20 minutes from initiation. Start at 1–2 mg/hr, increase by 1–2 mg/hr every 2 min. Example: start 2 \u2192 4 \u2192 6 \u2192 8 mg/hr = target reached in ~6 min. If max dose (21 mg/hr) doesn't achieve target, add another antihypertensive agent rather than exceeding the limit.

⚠️ REBOUND HYPERTENSION — Plan the Transition

Clevidipine's very short duration means BP will rebound within minutes of stopping. Rebound HTN is a listed adverse reaction. Before discontinuing, transition to a longer-acting agent (oral or IV). If rebound occurs, restart the infusion and establish a proper transition plan.

📋 NOT Weight-Based — Simple mg/hr Dosing

Unlike most LFN infusions, clevidipine is dosed in flat mg/hr (not mcg/kg/min). This simplifies pump programming but means the same rate applies regardless of patient size. A 50 kg patient and a 120 kg patient both start at 1–2 mg/hr. Titrate to clinical effect (target BP), not to a calculated dose.

🚫 CONTRAINDICATIONS (6 + Caution)

1. Hypersensitivity to drug/class/compound
2. Hypersensitivity to egg or egg products (lipid emulsion contains egg phospholipids)
3. Hypersensitivity to soy or soybean (lipid emulsion contains soybean oil)
4. Defective lipid metabolism (can't clear the lipid vehicle)
5. Acute pancreatitis w/hyperlipidemia (additional lipid load worsens condition)
6. Aortic stenosis, severe (afterload reduction = cardiovascular collapse)
Caution if heart failure (listed separately as caution, not absolute CI)

🚨 CRITICAL WARNING — Reflex Tachycardia + Beta-Blocker Rule

Reflex tachycardia may occur and may result in angina or MI in patients with obstructive coronary disease. In both situations: reduce clevidipine dose or discontinue if profound.

⚠️ DO NOT treat clevidipine-induced tachycardia with beta-blockers. This is a specific, testable LFN prohibition. The combination can cause refractory hypotension or cardiac arrest. Reduce the clevidipine rate instead.

🚫 Adverse Reactions (11)

Atrial fibrillation; MI; acute renal failure; hypersensitivity reaction; ileus; hypotension; headache; nausea; vomiting; tachycardia; rebound HTN. The most serious: MI (from reflex tachycardia in CAD), acute renal failure (from excessive BP reduction), and atrial fibrillation. Rebound HTN occurs after discontinuation without transition to longer-acting agent.
