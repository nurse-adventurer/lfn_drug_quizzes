🔬 Mechanism of Action (LFN verbatim)

"Stimulates smooth muscle alpha adrenergic receptors, producing vasoconstriction (sympathomimetic)."

Alpha-1 Stimulation
Binds alpha-1 adrenergic receptors on vascular smooth muscle — pure alpha-1, no beta activity listed
Vasoconstriction
Smooth muscle contraction → ↑ SVR → ↑ BP in shock/hypotension
Sympathomimetic
Mimics sympathetic nervous system vasoconstriction; classified as sympathomimetic in LFN MOA
No Beta Activity
No direct cardiac stimulation (beta-1); no bronchodilation (beta-2) — purely vascular alpha-1
📝 Onset Immediate / Peak 15–20 min / Duration Rapid Offset

The LFN 12.68 onset/peak/duration has a distinctive pattern: onset is immediate (vasoconstriction begins within seconds), but peak effect develops over 15–20 minutes. Duration is described as rapid offset — a qualitative descriptor rather than a specific time. This contrasts with norepinephrine 12.63 (onset 1–3 min, peak 5 min, duration 5–10 min). The 15–20 min peak means full BP response may take time to develop after a dose adjustment; rapid offset after stopping means hemodynamics will respond quickly to rate reductions.

🤰 Pregnancy Considerations

"Phenylephrine crosses the placenta. Phenylephrine injection may be used for the prevention and/or treatment of maternal hypotension."

Two key points: (1) Crosses placenta — fetal exposure occurs; inform receiving team. (2) Explicitly endorsed for maternal hypotension despite placental transfer — affirmative language ("may be used"), not merely "may be considered." Maternal hypotension (from shock, distributive causes, spinal-related) falls under the 12.68 indication of shock/hypotension.

🔴 No IV Bolus in LFN 12.68 — Infusion Only

LFN 12.68 lists only an infusion dose: 0.25–5 mcg/kg/min. There is no bolus dose in the LFN 12.68 formulary entry. The starting dose is 0.25 mcg/kg/min — not 0.5. This conservative lower starting point allows careful titration upward without overshooting. Any study question referencing an IV bolus dose for phenylephrine is importing content from non-LFN sources.

⚖️ Max 200 mcg/min — Flat Cap, Not Per-kg

The 200 mcg/min maximum is a flat-rate cap. For patients above 40 kg at full titration (5 mcg/kg/min), the weight-based calculation exceeds the cap. The cap then governs: give 200 mcg/min regardless of how large the patient is. This differs from norepinephrine 12.63, which lists both a flat cap (45 mcg/min) AND a per-kg max (2 mcg/kg/min).

🚫 CONTRAINDICATION (1)

Hypersensitivity — to phenylephrine or any component. Only one CI; the cardiac warning conditions (MI, arrhythmia, pulmonary edema) are cautionary warnings requiring use with caution, not absolute contraindications.

⚠️ Warnings / Precautions — Use with Caution (4)
Myocardial Infarction
↑ afterload → ↑ myocardial O2 demand → worsening ischemia or infarct extension in active MI
Tachyarrhythmia
↑ afterload → ↑ myocardial stress; BP elevation may trigger baroreceptor-mediated rhythm changes
Ventricular Arrhythmia
Pure vasoconstriction → ↑ wall tension in compromised ventricles → arrhythmia risk; no beta support
Pulmonary Edema
↑ SVR → ↑ LV afterload → potential worsening of pulmonary venous congestion in impaired LV

All four warnings reflect increased afterload without cardiac support. Not contraindications — phenylephrine may still be used with enhanced monitoring.

⚠️ Adverse Reactions (2)
Irregular Heartrate
Cardiac stress from increased afterload; encompasses dysrhythmias with pure vasoconstriction
Chest Tightness
↑ myocardial work from afterload elevation → chest discomfort or anginal equivalent

Critical distinction: Reflex bradycardia is an adverse reaction of norepinephrine 12.63, NOT phenylephrine 12.68. Tissue necrosis with extravasation also belongs to norepinephrine 12.63. LFN 12.68 adverse reactions are limited to two: irregular heartrate and chest tightness.
