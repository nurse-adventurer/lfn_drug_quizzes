🔬 Mechanism of Action

Relaxes vascular smooth muscle, resulting in dose-related dilation of both venous and arterial blood vessels. Promotes peripheral pooling of blood, reduction of peripheral resistance, and decreased venous return to the heart. Both LV preload and afterload are reduced, and myocardial oxygen consumption/demand is decreased.

🤰 Pregnancy

Nitroglycerin crosses the placenta. However, it may be used during pregnancy. The uterine inversion indication (50 mcg IV) is specifically an OB emergency use.

💊 SC #1 — PDE5 INHIBITOR INTERACTION: The Critical Time Windows

Do not administer to patients who have taken sildenafil within 24 hours OR vardenafil/tadalafil within 48 hours. PDE5 inhibitors (Viagra/Cialis/Levitra) potentiate NTG's vasodilatory effect → profound refractory hypotension. Different drugs = different half-lives = different time windows.

🫀 SC #2 — RIGHT-SIDED 12-LEAD: Inferior MI Screening

Obtain a right-sided 12-lead on patients presenting with an inferior MI. Inferior MI (ST elevation in II, III, aVF) may involve the right ventricle. Right ventricular MI is preload-dependent — NTG drops preload → hemodynamic collapse. The right-sided ECG (V4R) detects RV involvement before giving NTG.

📊 SC #3 — HYPOTENSION MANAGEMENT

Hypotension and bradycardia usually respond well to fluids and supine positioning. NTG-induced hypotension is from venodilation → decreased preload. Treatment: stop NTG + legs elevated + fluid bolus. Bradycardia is a Bezold-Jarisch reflex (vagal response to decreased preload). Both are usually self-limiting once NTG wears off (IV: 3-5 min; SL: ~25 min).

🫁 PULM EDEMA IV BOLUS — The Aggressive Option

For continued dyspnea due to pulmonary edema: 400 mcg over 2 min via IV pump. May repeat q 5 min × 3 total doses. SBP must be >120. This is a higher BP threshold because the 400 mcg bolus produces more profound vasodilation than SL or low-rate infusion. Per NIV pearl (PCG 8.23): consider initiating NTG infusion rather than removing mask to give SL tabs.

🚫 CONTRAINDICATIONS (3)

1. Hypersensitivity.
2. Recent PDE5 use — sildenafil within 24 hr OR vardenafil/tadalafil within 48 hr.
3. Alcohol and antihypertensive agents may compound hypotensive effects (potentiation CI).

⚠️ Warnings / Precautions (5)

Bradycardia; aortic stenosis; hypovolemia; increased ICP. Caution in patients with inferior/right-sided MI.

• Bradycardia: Bezold-Jarisch reflex from decreased preload
• Aortic stenosis: Fixed outflow obstruction + reduced preload → syncope/arrest
• Hypovolemia: Already low preload → NTG drops it further → shock
• Increased ICP: Venodilation can increase intracranial venous pressure
• Inferior/right-sided MI: RV preload-dependent → NTG → hemodynamic collapse

🚫 Adverse Reactions (5)

Hypotension; tachycardia; headache; blurred vision; vertigo.

• Hypotension: Primary dose-limiting effect — from venodilation
• Tachycardia: Reflex from hypotension (baroreceptor response)
• Headache: Most common — from meningeal vasodilation (up to 64% of patients). Often decreases with continued use.
• Blurred vision / vertigo: Cerebral vasodilation effects

💊 DRUG INTERACTION: NTG IV ↓ Heparin Activity

Per Heparin 12.35 Warnings: "Nitroglycerin IV may decrease anticoagulant activity." In STEMI patients receiving both NTG infusion and heparin, the heparin may be less effective. Monitor anticoagulation response and adjust heparin as needed.
